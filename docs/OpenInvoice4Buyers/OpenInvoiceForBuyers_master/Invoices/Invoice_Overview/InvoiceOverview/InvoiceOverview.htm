Invoice Overview

Invoices are created by suppliers after products and services have been delivered. OpenInvoice facilitates the process of creating and distributing invoices to buyers. This section provides an overview of invoices including how they are used in OpenInvoice as part of the business transaction workflow.

The benefits of submitting invoices electronically include:

·faster approval

·expedited invoice processing and timely payment

·ability to track the status and payment of each invoice

·online adjudication process for quick dispute resolution

·complete history and audit trail of all invoice-related changes

·no lost invoices

·mailing cost reduction.

Invoice Structure

An invoice consists of one or more line items (each line representing a product or service) that are coded according to the buyer’s accounts. The invoice view page includes an Invoice Journal, attachments, buyer and supplier information, referenced documents (such as price books), an invoice number, and other invoice details.

 

 

Invoice Journal

The Invoice Journal displays the current owner of the invoice and lists the actions that have been performed. The current owner is the site, department, and user (if applicable) responsible for processing the invoice. Each action is listed with the date and time of the action, the name of the user who performed the action, and, if applicable, comments entered at the time of performing the action. The action list displays the most recent action at the top and the oldest at the bottom.

Users will only see Invoice Journal information that is relevant to them and their company. For instance, buyers will see the supplier’s Submitted action along with any buyer actions. The journal includes Current Owner information and the actions performed on the invoice.

 

 

Journal Items Visible to Buyers

A buyer can view the following items in the Invoice Journal:

·All actions performed on the invoice by users in their company.

·All actions performed on the invoice by the system.

·Names of users within their company who have performed an action on the invoice.

·Names of supplier users who have performed an action on the invoice.

·Comments made at the time of invoice submission and dispute.

·Comments made at the time of forwarding by a user within their company.

·General comments entered using the invoice’s toolbar by users within their company.

·Payment actions related to the invoice if the company is using the OpenInvoice Payment module.

·Payment status updates related to the invoice submitted through integration if the company is not using the payment module.

Journal Items Visible to Suppliers

A supplier can view the following items in the Invoice Journal:

·All actions performed on the invoice by users in their company.

·All actions performed on the invoice by the system.

·Names of users within their company who have performed an action on the invoice.

·Name of the buyer user who disputed the invoice to the supplier.

·The internal dispute action is not visible to the supplier.

·Comments made at the time of invoice submission and dispute.

·Comments made at the time of forwarding by a user within their company.

·General comments entered using the invoice’s toolbar by users within their company.

·Approved: by Buyer is displayed when the invoice is approved.

·Itemized: by Buyer is displayed when the Image Scan invoice is itemized by the buyer.

·Payment status updates related to the invoice.

Workflow Actions Visible in the Invoice Journal

Note:For a description of workflow actions related to payments, refer to the OpenInvoice Payment User Guide for Buyers.

·Saved: a user within your company modified the buyer editable invoice content.

·Submitted: a supplier user, one of your indexers, or OpenInvoice Support submitted the invoice for approval. This includes resubmissions. If an image was uploaded on the supplier’s behalf, the uploader’s name is displayed as a comment along with the date and time when the image was uploaded.

Note:Any actions performed by OpenInvoice Support will display as by OpenInvoice Indexer to users within your company. Any actions performed by OpenInvoice Support or one of your indexers on behalf of a supplier will display as by Buyer when a supplier views the invoice.

·Forwarded: a user within your company forwarded the invoice to another user within your company. The name of the user who performed the action and the recipient are both listed. If the invoice is forwarded to a department, the site and department name are listed. If the invoice is forwarded as a result of invoice recipient routing, the System is listed as the user who performed the action. 

·Commented: a user within your company provided additional information about the invoice.

·Disputed: a user within your company disagreed with information on the invoice and returned it to the supplier for resolution. Once the supplier cancels or resubmits the invoice, users can click Disputed to display the disputed version of the invoice. The HISTORICAL watermark indicates the invoice is not the current version. If the invoice is auto-disputed, the System is listed as the user who performed the action.

·Disputed Internally: a user within your company who disagreed with information on the invoice disputed the invoice. Your company’s configured dispute management rules routed the invoice to a department within your company to resolve instead of returning it back to the supplier. A system generated comment stating the department the invoice was routed to is added to the action. This action and accompanying comment is not visible to the supplier.

·Coding Verified: a user within your company verified the coding on the invoice. If the invoice is auto code-verified, the System will be listed as the user who performed the action.

·Approved: the invoice received an approval signature from a user within your company. Multiple approval actions may be required to change the invoice status to Approved. If the invoice is forwarded to a department, the site and department name are listed. If the invoice is auto-approved, the System is listed as the user who performed the action.

·Cancelled: a supplier user or the system cancelled an invoice. If you have configured the system to automatically cancel disputed invoices after a defined number of days with no supplier action, the System is listed as the user who performed the action and a comment stating the number of days is added to the action.

·Itemized: a user added or modified detail lines on an image invoice.

·Corrected: on behalf of a supplier, a user within your company edited the header information of an Image Post or Image OBO invoice. Any comments made at the time of editing are listed. If the supplier name or supplier site were modified, the original and modified values are displayed.

·Short Paid: a user within your company reduced the Remit Amount on an Image OBO or Image Post invoice.

·Held and Hold Released: the invoice is held after a user within your company added a new supplier and setup is incomplete. The invoice is view-only. Once a user with the Corporate Partnership permission enters/verifies the supplier’s information, the hold is released.

·Delegation Confirmed: a user within your company performed an action on the invoice that was delegated to them by another user. Invoice ownership has been transferred to the delegate.

·Workflow set to Client: automatic export by the system or manual export performed by a user within your company. Automatic export to the buyer’s ERP is performed according to the invoice workflow rules. In the case of a manual export, the invoice will be exported to the buyer’s ERP when the Rules Based Invoice Export scheduled service is run. The date/time and name of the user who exported the invoice will be displayed. The System will be listed as the user who exported the invoice if it was automatically exported.

·Workflow set to OpenInvoice: a user within your company moved the invoice from the Client Workflow to the OpenInvoice Workflow. The date/time and name of the user who unlocked the invoice is displayed.

·Unapproved: a user within your company returned a previously approved invoice back into the workflow for issue resolution. The invoice reverts to the status it had prior to being approved. The comment associated with this action is not visible to suppliers.

Invoice Ownership

Invoices are submitted to a site’s Remit To department, which then becomes the invoice owner. From there, invoices can be forwarded to another department and/or user. Any user with the appropriate permissions can work on a department-owned invoice.

A user becomes the owner of an invoice if it has been forwarded to them or if they perform a workflow action that does not require them to forward the invoice for additional processing (final financial approval in the Code then Approve workflow). If an invoice has been automatically processed according to your company’s invoice workflow rules, the owner on the invoice will depend on the workflow sequence your company is using.

Unless your company has configured invoice workflow rules to automate actions performed on an invoice, only the owner or the owner’s delegate can process it.

You may forward an invoice to users who have the ability to view, modify, or perform the next workflow step on the invoice. If the next workflow step is financial approval and the user is able to financially approve the invoice, the user’s authorization levels will be displayed in the user list on the forwarding page. Once the invoice is forwarded, ownership of the invoice is transferred and the original owner of the invoice can no longer process it. A user with the Invoice Admin permission can forward an invoice on behalf of an invoice owner.

Suppliers can send invoice messages to a buyer, but only the buyer company is listed as the recipient in the message. On the buyer’s side, the message is sent to the invoice owner. If the invoice is owned by a department, the department’s notification user receives the message.

Delegations

An invoice owner who will be away for a period of time may delegate their ownership to another user who has the same viewing permissions (at least the Invoice View permission in the same site as the original invoice owner). Invoice ownership is transferred once the delegate performs an action (Save, Forward, Code Verify, Dispute or Approve) on the delegated invoice. Transfer of invoice ownership is displayed in the Invoice Journal.

Things to Consider when Delegating Invoice Ownership

·Delegation does not transfer permissions or authorization levels. The delegate will be able to forward an invoice, but their ability to perform other invoice functions will depend on their assigned permissions and authorization level; the final owner of a forwarded invoice is the recipient.

·If the supplier has sent a message on a delegated invoice, the delegate does not need to perform an action before receiving a copy of the message. The original invoice owner will still receive the supplier’s messages until the delegate replies.

Note:Replying to the supplier’s message will not transfer ownership of the invoice.

·Ensure the delegate is available on the days that the invoice owner will be away. A delegate cannot delegate their own invoices or re-delegate another user’s invoices.

Where Ownership is Displayed

In the Invoice Journal, the Current Owner section will show the destination site, department, and user’s name (if owned by a user) of the last submit or forward action.

The user’s number of outstanding invoices displays in yellow at the top of their profile page.

Attached Documents

Attached documents are found next to the Invoice Journal. In the Attached Documents area, an attachment is listed with a date, time, attachment description, and user who attached the document. The most recent attached document is listed at the bottom and the oldest is at the top. View an attachment by clicking the attachment description. Delete an attachment by clicking the Delete icon.

Buyer and Supplier Information

The original buyer and supplier companies are listed here, even if the invoice is routed or forwarded to another buyer department or site after submission.

General Information

The details listed on an invoice are determined by the configuration of the invoice data fields by the buyer.

·Invoice Number: must be unique for each supplier. This number may be automatically generated by the system or manually entered by the supplier.

·Masked Invoice Number: invoice numbers that have been modified to have a maximum length of 16 characters and contain only alphanumeric characters (A-Z, 0-9). The Masked Invoice Number displays if the buyer has enabled invoice number masking.

·Invoice Type: specifies whether the invoice has a positive total (Original Invoice) or negative total (Credit Invoice).

·Submission Type: specifies how the invoice was submitted.

·Status: specifies the invoice’s location in the workflow.

·Routing Code: used to automatically forward the invoice to a buyer’s department and/or user for processing when the invoice is submitted. A buyer can disable this field.

·Supplier Number: the number assigned to the supplier.

·Workflow: the current workflow state of the invoice.

·Invoice Date: supplier specified value. This date is typically the invoice creation date in the supplier’s accounting system. The date is entered in MM/DD/YYYY format.

·Invoice Due Date: The date the invoice is due.

·Service Date: specify the time period when the work was performed. The Service Date must be on or before the Invoice Date. This field will default to the invoice date.

·Currency: the currency applicable to the invoice.

·Invoice Amount: total amount due to the supplier. This amount includes the sum of all invoice lines and taxes.

·Remit Amount: total payment requested after credits are applied.

·Self Assess Tax: indicates if the self assess tax should be applied.

·Quick Pay: also known as Priority Pay. Priority Pay agreements are made outside of the OpenInvoice Platform and, as such, remit amounts calculated in OpenInvoice are not adjusted.

·Description: describes the charges/credits listed on the invoice.

·WO: the work order reference.

·PO: the purchase order number reference.

Price Book and Early Pay Information

When the buyer and supplier have negotiated pricing and payment agreements, these agreements can be applied to invoices. If an invoice includes Early Pay Terms, the invoice must be paid by the specified Early Pay due date for the Early Pay discount to apply.

Line Item Details and Coding

Line item details are determined by the configuration of the invoice data fields by the buyer. Line items may have specific coding requirements that must be met before the invoice can proceed through the workflow. Some fields may require entries in additional fields (entering an AFE may result in the Cost Center, Location, Field/Lease, Major, and Minor fields being populated).

Each line will have one or more coding records. At a minimum, there is one coding record for the non-tax portion of the invoice line and one coding record for each tax applied to the invoice line. Coding can be copied and applied to multiple line items.

An invoice’s coding history can be viewed by clicking the Coding History link above the Coding section. The Coding History displays the coding information for the invoice line item at the time the invoice was submitted, including the Smart Coded custom data fields and the source for the GL codes.

·Service Date or Service Date From/To: populated according to the service date displayed in the header. Buyers can configure whether a single date or date range is displayed. If the service date is a range, Service Date From is used to filter price book products/services and determine price book compliance.

Calculating Invoice Line Amounts

All invoice line amounts are calculated by OpenInvoice, including the amounts on invoices submitted from B2B suppliers. The precision and rounding methods used by OpenInvoice may be different than your financial system. As a result, you may see a difference between the totals calculated in OpenInvoice and the totals on the backup provided by the supplier.

Input fields (Quantity, Rate, etc.) have a precision of 2, 6, or 8 decimal points. Calculated fields (Subtotal, Discount Amount, etc.) have a precision of 2 or 10 decimal points.

Calculations

Note:The following calculations are performed for each invoice line. The result of each calculation step is rounded to its defined precision and the rounded value is used as the input for subsequent calculations.

Steps:

1.Subtotal = Quantity * Rate

2.Discount Rate = Discount Percent / 100

3.Discount Amount = Subtotal * Discount Rate

4.Pre-tax Amount = Subtotal – Discount Amount

5.Tax Rate = Tax Percent / 100

6.Tax Amount = Pre-tax Amount * Tax Rate

Note:This is calculated separately for each tax type applied to the line.

7.Total = Pre-tax Amount + Sum (Tax Amount (for all taxes on the line))

If the invoice line is eligible for an Early Pay discount, the discount is calculated as:

1.Early Pay Discount Rate = Early Pay Discount Percentage / 100

2.Early Pay Discount Amount = (Pre-tax Amount + Sum (EP eligible Tax Amounts)) * Early Pay Discount Rate

3.Early Pay Total = Total – Early Pay Discount Amount

Displayed Values

When viewing the details of each invoice line item, the Total field represents the line’s total after discounts and taxes are applied. See the previous section for a breakdown of the Total calculation.

On the invoice’s Summary tab, the Total column represents each invoice line’s total after discounts but before taxes. See the previous section for a breakdown of the Pre-Tax Amount calculation.

The invoice’s Total Amount is displayed at the bottom of the Summary tab and is calculated by adding the sum of each line’s tax amounts to the sum of the Total column.

Converting Dollar Values to Percentages

When a B2B supplier submits an invoice specifying either a discount or tax as a dollar value instead of a percentage, OpenInvoice calculates the percentage discount or tax, which is stored in the OpenInvoice database. The stored percentage is used to calculate and display the dollar amount in all future calculations.

Price Reconciliations

All price reconciliations are performed using the discounted rate. A discounted rate on the invoice line will reconcile with:

·The referenced purchase order line if the discounted rate equals the rate on the purchase order line.

·The referenced ticket if the discounted rate equals the discounted rate on the ticket.

·The referenced price book if the discounted rate is between the adjusted rates on the price book.

Early Pay Terms and Discount

Buyer administrators have the option to configure taxes as Eligible for Early Pay Discounts. If the tax is eligible for Early Pay discounts and the invoice is approved with an Early Pay discount, the tax and the product/service amount is discounted. If the tax is not eligible for Early Pay discounts, just the product/service amount is discounted and the full tax amount still applies.

For example, assume the invoice line is $1000 with a tax rate of 10% for a total of $1100. If there is a 5% Early Pay discount and the tax is eligible then the amount due is $1100 – 5% = $1045. If the tax is not eligible for the discount, the amount due is ($1000 – 5%) + (tax of $100) = $1050.

Early Pay Program Rules

This section describes the rules associated with the Early Pay Program.

The EPP rules are:

·Only available to OpenInvoice Suppliers.

·Payment terms are based on the invoice submission date.

·Correctly submitted invoices only are applicable to the EP Program.

Note:If an invoice is disputed, corrected, and re-submitted the payment terms are based on the original invoice submission date not the re-submitted invoice date.

·Enter the original invoice costs, the EP functionality will calculate the discount and factor it in.

·The EP discount is calculated automatically on all lines of the invoice where the EP program has been applied by the supplier.

·If an invoice is approved after the EP deadline, the system marks the invoice as “EP not realized” and the invoice total remains the full total, without the EP discount.

Alerts Presented on an Invoice

The following alerts are presented on an invoice:

·Price book, purchase order, duplicate ticket, and ticket compliance alerts

·Early Pay, Priority Pay, and image invoice information alerts

The compliance alerts are displayed on the My Ready to Approve portlet of the Invoice Dashboard, list pages (if configured to display alerts), the invoice Summary tab and the invoice Coding tab. The information alerts are only displayed on the My Ready to Approve portlet of the Invoice Dashboard and list pages (if configured to display alerts).

Compliance alerts are evaluated on all unapproved invoices. Once invoices are approved, the alert statuses are maintained and no longer evaluated. For example, a green alert at the time of the invoice’s approval will remain as a green alert after the invoice is approved and will never change. For descriptions of the compliance alerts, refer to Price Book Compliance Alerts and Purchase Order Compliance Alerts.

Invoice Submission Types and Guidelines

OpenInvoice will assign the following submission types to invoices depending on how the invoice is created and submitted. Submission types control the actions that can be performed and determine additional processing logic that occurs during the submission process.

·Direct Entry

§Created by the supplier who manually enters the invoice header and line details, creates the invoice from a purchase order, or creates the invoice from tickets.

·LIDO CSV / LIDO XML

§Created by the supplier who uploads a CSV or XML file containing the invoice header and line details. Once the file is uploaded, the supplier can modify the invoice content and/or add attachments prior to submitting the invoice.

·B2B

§Created by the supplier who transmits an XML file containing the invoice header and line details. The XML file can include any associated attachments.

·Image Scan

§Created by the supplier who uploads a scanned copy of the invoice, enters basic header information, and optionally itemizes the invoice.

§These invoices can be itemized by either the supplier or buyer.

§These invoices cannot be short paid.

·Image OBO

§Created by a buyer user working on behalf of a supplier. The buyer uploads a scanned copy of the invoice, enters basic header information, and optionally itemizes the invoice.

§These invoices can be itemized at any point in the invoice workflow.

§The Correct function can be used to modify the invoice header information.

§These invoices can be short paid.

·Image Post

§Created by the buyer transmitting an XML file with a scanned attachment and basic header.

§These invoices can be itemized at any point in the invoice workflow.

§These invoices cannot be disputed to the supplier; however, they can be disputed internally to a department that is configured to manage and/or resolve dispute issues.

§The Correct function can be used to modify the invoice header information.

§These invoices can be short paid.

·Transaction Upload

§Created by a buyer user in the platform or by transmitting a file via integration.

§Represents transactions that were previously approved and/or paid outside of OpenInvoice.

§The transactions can be associated with any enabled suppler (registered or unregistered).

§The transactions are imported with an Approved or Paid invoice status.

§The transactions cannot be unapproved.

§Qualifying transactions imported with an Approved status may be paid using OpenInvoice.

Invoice Dashboard

The Invoice Dashboard is accessed by going to Invoice > Dashboard. The dashboard consists of five portlets:

·Invoices: displays counts and provides direct access to invoices you are responsible for processing.

·Field Tickets: displays the My Ready to Approve tickets if you have the Field Ticket Modify or Field Ticket Modify (Restricted) permissions.

·Invoice Counts: (Invoice Admin permissions only) displays the Ready to Code and Ready to Approve invoices organized by date range calculated using the Last Submit Date. You can filter this data by Site and Currency.

Note:Users with Corporate Admin permissions can change the date range calculation to use the Last Action Date.

·My Ready to Approve: displays a list of invoices that require your financial approval.

·Purchase Orders: displays counts and provides direct access to the purchase orders that you are responsible for processing. For an explanation of the Purchase Orders portlet, refer to Purchase Orders Portlet.

·Average Time To Process: displays a graphical representation of the organization’s complete workflow performance over the past three months.

·Completed Reports: displays the current user’s completed reports.

 

Invoices Portlet

The Invoices portlet summarizes your invoices by workflow status. The portlet only provides access to invoices that you are able to process in the OpenInvoice platform based on your assigned permissions and authorization levels. Invoices routed to your ERP for client processing are excluded from the portlet.

Your invoices are categorized into the following columns:

·Early Pay: invoices that are currently eligible for an Early Pay discount.

·Count: invoices that are in a Submitted or Resubmitted status (including those in the Early Pay and Stalled columns).

·Stalled: invoices considered stalled based on the Days to Stalled Invoices After Last Action company preference.

The rows of the portlet represent the invoice workflow status:

·My Custom List (Configure): a saved search that can be accessed through the dashboard for convenience. Once My Custom List is set up, there will be a View link that generates the search results. Click the link to generate a previously configured list.

·Supplier Setup: a holding queue for posted invoices from a supplier who requires setup in OpenInvoice. This category is not displayed when the count is 0.

·Duplicate Checking: a holding queue for posted invoices with invoice numbers that have already been used by the supplier in OpenInvoice. The category is displayed only if the current user has the Invoice Hold Adjudication permission.

·Ready to Code: invoices that are ready to be coded by someone in your department who has the Invoice Modify permission. The category is not displayed when the count is identical to the My Ready to Code count (the user owns all invoices in the department).

·My Ready to Code: invoices that have been submitted or forwarded to you for coding. This category is not displayed when the count is 0.

·Ready to Review: invoices that are ready to be reviewed by someone in the departments where you have either the Invoice Modify or the Invoice Approve permission, but you do not have an authorization level to financially approve invoices. The category is not displayed when the count is identical to the My Ready to Review count (the user owns all invoices in the department).

·My Ready to Review: invoices that are ready for your review after coding, but you cannot financially approve because you do not have the appropriate authorization level. The category is not displayed when the count is 0.

·Ready to Approve: invoices that are ready to be approved by someone in the departments where you have either the Invoice Modify or the Invoice Approve permission and you do have an authorization level allowing you to financially approve invoices. The category is not displayed when the count is identical to the My Ready to Approve count (the user owns all invoices in the department). If coding and review are performed by another user, ensure you have the appropriate authorization level for the category to be included in the portlet.

·My Ready to Approve: invoices that are ready for your approval. This category is not displayed when the count is 0. If coding and review are performed by another user, ensure you have the appropriate authorization level for the category to be included in the portlet.

·Delegated to Me: invoices, owned by another user, that have been delegated to you. This category appears in the portlet if you have at least Invoice View permission in one site and you have been delegated at least one active invoice.

·My Recent Approvals: invoices that you final approved within the last 30 days. The category is not displayed when the count is 0.

·Notification User: stalled invoices based on the Days to Stalled Invoice After Last Action company preference. You are the notification user for the department of the stalled invoice. The category is not displayed when the count is 0.

·Disputes to Resolve: invoices that have been disputed to an internal department for resolution instead of being disputed back to the supplier or indexer. This category is not displayed when the count is 0.

·Unowned Invoices (Invoice Admin permission only): invoices that are owned by users who no longer have access to the OpenInvoice Platform or by a department that no longer has any active users.

Field Tickets Portlet

The Field Tickets portlet displays the number of field tickets that are ready for your approval. This portlet will display field tickets that are Submitted and you are: the requisitioner, the ticket owner, or have Field Ticket Modify (Restricted) permissions for the department that is the ticket owner. Click on the count displayed to view the list of all field tickets awaiting approval.

My Ready to Approve Portlet

The My Ready to Approve portlet lists your invoices that are in the financial approval portion of workflow. The portlet displays each invoice’s original submit date, alerts, the supplier company name, the invoice number, the invoice amount, the total of all line item discounts on the invoice, and the total of all taxes on the invoice. The portlet provides you the ability to approve, dispute, forward, or comment on one or more invoices according to your permissions, authorization levels, and the company configuration. Click the Invoice # to open the invoice.

Invoice Counts Portlet

This portlet displays if you have Invoice Admin permissions. The Invoice Counts portlet displays the invoices that are Ready to Code and Ready to Approve. The invoices are sorted into date ranges. These date ranges are calculated using the Last Submit Date. Users with Corporate Admin permissions can change the calculation to use Last Action Date if desired in the Company Preferences.  

Purchase Orders Portlet

The Purchase Orders Portlet displays counts and provides direct access to purchase orders that the user is responsible for processing.

Average Time to Process Portlet

The Average Time to Process portlet provides you with a graphical overview of the invoicing processing performance for the entire company. The portlet includes three months of data and the averages for all invoices processed by the company, not just the invoices the you processed. For each month, the graph calculates the average time for the buyer to code verify an invoice, for the buyer to financially approve an invoice, and for a supplier to resubmit a disputed invoice. The graph also displays the average time from initial submission to completion of all workflow steps resulting in invoice approval.

Hover over one of the bar graphs to display the month, total number of invoices included in the average, and the average number of days.

The averages for the past year are displayed directly below the graph. The past year averages include the previous 12 months of data plus the current month. For example, if viewing the graph on May 27, 2013, the past year average includes all data from May 1, 2012 to May 27, 2013.

The Completed Reports Portlet

The Completed Reports portlet provides you with your list of completed reports. Click the subject to open or save the report. The portlet includes the date that the report will expire and be automatically deleted.

Invoice Search and My Custom List

Searches for invoices are performed using one of the following ways: specifying multiple search criteria or, if all or part of an invoice number is known, using the Global Search field. To specify multiple search criteria, use the Invoice Search function, which also allows users to save search criteria (My Custom List).

Search Details

Additional detailed search fields are available in three sections: Invoice Details, Line Item Details, and Coding Details. The Coding Details section displays if you select any search criteria in the Line Item Details section of the search screen.

How to Search by Invoice Number

Use this procedure to search by invoice number from the Global Search field.

Permission:Invoice View

Steps:

1.In the main navigation bar, click the down arrow in the Global Search field and select Invoice #.

 

2.Enter all or part of the invoice number.

Note:If only part of the invoice number is known, an asterisk (*) must be used to denote the unknown part of the invoice number.

If your company is using invoice number masking, you can search by either the masked invoice number or the original invoice number entered by the supplier.

As you type the invoice number, a list of up to 20 matching invoices is displayed in a quick-view list. The list is based on the most recent invoice dates, then sorted by supplier name. The invoice number, supplier name, submission date, currency, total, and status of the invoice are also displayed.

3.Click the Search icon to select from the resulting list page or select an invoice from the quick-view list.

Note:Selecting an invoice from the quick-view list will open a read-only version of the invoice in a new window.      

How to Search for an Invoice by Multiple Criteria

Use this procedure to search for an invoice using multiple search criteria.

Permission:Invoice View

Steps:

1.Select Invoice > Invoice Search.

Note:The criteria entered on your last search will populate the fields. Click Reset Criteria to clear all fields.

2.Select how your search results are displayed with the Select Columns, Number of Results, and View Results As options.

Depending on the option selected in the Select Columns drop-down, additional criteria may be automatically applied to your search. For example, selecting Ready to Code will limit the results to just the invoices in the code verification portion of the workflow.

 

3.Enter additional search criteria in the Invoice Details or Line Item Details sections.

Note:In some fields, you may type an asterisk (*) and click the Search icon next to the field to reveal a list of available options. Select an option and click Submit.

For the Supplier Name field, use whole words without an asterisk (*) when selecting the Words Within Name matching option. For the Name matching option, use the entire supplier name or partial name followed by an *.

4.Click Find Invoices or Run Report, depending on your View Result As selection.

Configuring the Search Output

·Select Columns: determines which columns are displayed in the search results. The options available in this drop-down list depend on your permissions and whether or not you have an authorization level.

§Invoice Search: returns a list of invoices that match the selected criteria.

§Invoice Reporting: returns a list of invoices matching the selected criteria for reporting.

§Invoice Administration: returns a list of invoices matching the selected criteria for departments in which you have the Invoice Admin permission.

§Ready to Code: returns invoices that are ready for code verification and match the selected criteria.

§Ready to Approve: returns invoices ready for financial approval and match the selected criteria.

§Ready to Review: returns invoices ready for your review.

§Disputes to Resolve: returns disputed invoices requiring resolution.

§Delegated Invoices: returns delegated invoices that match the selected criteria.

§Duplicate Checking: returns invoices with duplicate numbers that match the selected criteria.

§Custom: only applies to saved custom reports.

Note:Click Customize next to the drop-down list to modify your saved configured list settings for the selected column configuration.

·Number of results: only the first 1000 qualifying invoices are returned.

·View Results As: displays your search results in one of the following forms:

§List: on a new page as a list.

§Summary Report: a spreadsheet report that can be accessed from the completed reports icon or Completed Reports portlet. Each row in the spreadsheet will display an invoice.

§Detailed Report: a spreadsheet report that can be accessed from the Completed Reports icon or Completed Reports portlet. Each row in the spreadsheet will display an invoice coding record along with its header and other line information.

Note:The Current Status field allows you to search for invoices with a specific status. The options are:

-All: includes all invoices

-All Except Disputed: includes invoices where the current status is not Disputed.

-All Unapproved: includes only the unapproved invoices.

-On-Hold: includes only the invoices on-hold.

-Approved: includes only the approved invoices.

-Disputed: includes only the disputed invoices.

-Cancelled: includes only the cancelled invoices.

-Re-Submitted: includes only the re-submitted invoices.

-Submitted: includes only the submitted invoices.

-Submitted and Re-Submitted: includes the submitted and re-submitted invoices.

-Paid: includes only the paid invoices.

-Approved, not yet Paid: includes the invoices that have been approved but not yet paid.

The following invoice configurable list options include multiple columns when added to lists and reports:

·Coding: includes the Major, Minor, and Subcode columns for detail reports.

·Description:

How to Save Customized Search Criteria

Use this procedure to save your customized search criteria.

Permission:Invoice View

Steps:

1.Select Invoice > Dashboard.

2.In the Invoices portlet, click Configure in the My Custom List row.

 

3.Enter your search criteria.

4.Depending on your View Result As selection, click Find Invoices or Run Report.      

How to Search Based on Saved Customized Search Criteria

Use this procedure to search using your previously saved customized search criteria.

Permission:Invoice View

Steps:

1.Select Invoice > Dashboard.

2.In the Invoices portlet, click View in the My Custom List row.

Note:The View link will not be available until a customized search has been saved.      

 

Invoice List Pages

Invoice list pages are generated when you perform an invoice search or click an item on the dashboard. The invoices you can select and view from the list page will depend on your search criteria, your permissions, and the invoice status.You can click a column heading to sort the results by that column.

The first column will be highlighted yellow if the invoice is considered stalled. An invoice is stalled when no action has been performed on the invoice within the defined Days to Stalled Invoice After Last Action company preference value. The column will be red if the invoice is considered stalled because it has not been approved within the defined Days to Stalled Invoice After Most Recent Submit company preference value.

Note:If an invoice is considered stalled based on both preferences, the Days to Stalled Invoice Most Recent Submit preference takes priority and the first column on the list page will be red.

After opening an invoice from the list page you can:

·move to the previous or next invoice without navigating back to the list page. Click the Previous In List and Next In List links located at the top of the page.

·use the breadcrumb to return to the Invoice list page.

·process the invoice. The next invoice on the list page will automatically open. If you have processed the last invoice in the list, you will be returned to the Invoice List page.
