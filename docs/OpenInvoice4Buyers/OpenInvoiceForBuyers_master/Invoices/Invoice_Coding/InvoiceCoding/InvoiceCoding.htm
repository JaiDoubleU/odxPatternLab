Invoice Coding

Coding is the process of allocating the costs (expenses) associated with invoices to the appropriate cost objects and general ledger codes.

The following topics are applicable to users who review invoices for approval:

·Automatic Invoice Coding

·Invoice Coding Page Overview

·Split Coding

·Check Coding

Coding Process

·Coding is added to line items and verified before the invoice is approved for payment. Depending on the workflow your company uses, this may be before or after the financial approval process.

·Based on your company configuration, suppliers may add cost objects and general ledger coding to the invoice prior to submitting the invoice. If the supplier enters values on an invoice that do not exist in the platform, they will be removed when the invoice is submitted. OpenInvoice does not notify the buyer or supplier when invalid coding is removed.

·During the invoice submission process, OpenInvoice will attempt to automatically apply cost objects and general ledger coding to the invoice based on your configuration, the documents (field tickets and/or purchase order) linked to the invoice, and/or the supplier entered values.

Coding Business Rules

·Your company administrator has configured the chart of accounts and business rules to determine when the cost objects, general ledger coding, field/lease, asset, location, operational categories, and purchase order information are required and the relationships between their data values. You can enter and save data that violates the rules, but you will not be able to code verify the invoice until the invoice passes all rules.

·A tax can be mapped to a general ledger code so the code is automatically applied to the line for the tax. The general ledger codes applied to mapped taxes cannot be modified and additional cost objects cannot be added to the tax.

Automatic Invoice Coding

As suppliers submit invoices, either through integration or by OpenInvoice entry, the platform will attempt to apply cost objects (AFE and cost center) and general ledger coding (major, minor and subcode) values to the invoice’s lines. The AFE, cost center, and general ledger coding fields are all processed independently of one another, so an invoice line may take the AFE from one source and general ledger coding from another, for example. Any coding that is applied from a field ticket, purchase order, or supplier is validated before the platform submits the invoice.

The platform will give priority to cost object and general ledger coding values you have previously reviewed or provided for the specific invoice. If your company has enabled supplier sites with the Enable Smart Coding setting, the platform will attempt to use general ledger coding values from previously approved or code verified invoices with the same attributes.

·The major/minor/subcode string must be a valid chart of accounts mapping in your platform.

·If the company preference Parse Format for Supplier Coding is enabled, the general ledger coding on invoices submitted through integration must adhere to the configured format.

The following diagram illustrates the platform’s process of determining the initial coding source when an invoice is created. The process also includes general ledger coding validity and how to populate missing general ledger coding during the invoice submission process.

Cost Objects

1.Field Ticket: If the invoice line refers to a field ticket, the platform will try to apply the AFE and cost center on the field ticket line.

2.Purchase Order: If the invoice line does not refer to a field ticket or the field ticket line does not include a cost object, the platform will try to apply the cost objects on the purchase order line referenced on the invoice line.

Note:If the supplier modifies the PO and PO line reference on an invoice line, the cost objects on the PO line will overwrite the cost objects on the invoice line regardless of whether the invoice line refers to a field ticket or not.

3.Supplier (user interface): If the supplier modifies the AFE and/or cost center from the values taken from the field ticket or purchase order, the supplier entered values are submitted to the buyer. The platform does not revert the values back to the values on the field ticket or purchase order.

Supplier (integration): If the invoice line does not refer to a field ticket or purchase order line or the referenced document does not include cost objects, the platform uses the AFE and/or cost center contained in the invoice file. The invoice file may contain cost objects in the header or on the individual lines. The platform will use the cost objects on the individual lines first. If there is no cost object on a line, the cost object in the header will be applied.

4.The platform validates the applied AFE and cost center. If they do not exist on the buyer’s side, they are removed. If valid cost objects cannot be found in any of the above sources, the invoice line is submitted to the buyer without cost objects.

General Ledger Coding

1.Field Ticket: If the invoice refers to a field ticket, the platform will try to apply the general ledger coding on the field ticket line.

2.Purchase Order: If the invoice does not refer to a field ticket or the field ticket does not include general ledger coding, the platform will try to apply the general ledger coding on the purchase order line referenced on the invoice line.

Note:If the supplier modifies the PO and PO line reference on an invoice line, the general ledger coding on the PO line will overwrite the general ledger coding on the invoice line regardless of whether the invoice line refers to a field ticket or not.

3.Supplier (user interface): If the supplier modifies the general ledger coding from the values taken from the field ticket or purchase order line, the supplier entered values are submitted to the buyer. The platform does not revert the values back to the values on the field ticket or purchase order.

Note:The supplier configuration setting Allow Supplier Coding must be selected for the supplier to view or modify the general ledger codes.

Supplier (integration): If the invoice does not refer to a field ticket or purchase order line, or the referenced document does not include general ledger coding and the supplier configuration setting Allow Supplier Coding is selected, the platform uses the general ledger coding contained in the invoice file. The invoice file may contain general ledger coding in the header or on the individual lines. The platform will try to use the general ledger coding on the individual lines first. If there is no general ledger coding on a line, the general ledger coding in the header will be applied. If the supplier configuration setting Allow Supplier Coding is not selected, any general ledger coding from the supplier will be ignored.

4.The platform validates the applied general ledger coding. If the general ledger coding is not a valid combination in the chart of accounts, it is removed.

5.Previously Approved Invoice: If the supplier configuration setting Enable Smart Coding is selected and the invoice does not have general ledger coding, the platform will try to apply the general ledger coding from the last approved invoice or last code verified invoice with the same configured attributes.  

6.If valid general ledger coding cannot be found in any of the above sources, the invoice line is submitted to the buyer with no general ledger coding applied.

Invoice Coding Page Overview

An invoice can be coded from the Coding tab when viewing an invoice. This tab includes an allocation summary (if applicable) and invoice fields to enter values that can be applied to multiple line items. Visible fields will depend on your company administrator’s configurations. The Coding tab also directs users to the Split Coding function and coding templates.

Note:On the Coding and Split Coding pages the type-ahead functionality works with the AFE and Cost Center fields. The results list is filtered by the AFE/Cost Center and Properties.      

 

·Allocation Summary: may include AFE, cost center, location, field/lease, major/minor/subcode, asset number, purchase order, work order, and custom reference data fields. A column is only displayed if it is applied to at least one line item on the invoice. The coding records are grouped according to all of the fields and the amount is the sum of the coding records for each group.

·Split: allows the coder to split the costs associated with a single invoice line across multiple cost object and general ledger coding combinations.

·Select Template: reduces data entry by saving and applying a copy of frequently used cost allocations.

·Line Items: include product/service description, quantity, unit of measure, rate, and any applicable discounts.

Applying Coding to Coding Records

You can apply coding to coding records in two ways: manually, or by copying existing coding from a record.

How to Code an Invoice Manually

Permission:Invoice Modify

Steps:

1.Do one of the following:

§Select Invoice > Dashboard and click one of the dashboard counters.

§Perform a search by Invoice # using the Global Search field in the main navigation bar.

§Perform a search with multiple criteria by going to Invoice > Invoice Search.

2.Click the Invoice #.

3.Click the Coding tab.

4.Enter coding information.

Note:If only part of a value is known, type an asterisk (*) into the field and click the lookup icon.

 

5.Select the checkbox next to the line items you want to apply the coding.

6.Click Apply to Selected.

7.Click Save on the toolbar.

Referencing a Purchase Order and Purchase Order Line

·A line item may be associated with a purchase order and purchase order line. By default, if valid purchase orders exist in OpenInvoice for the supplier, the referenced purchase order must be one of the purchase orders in platform.

·A quick-view list of matching purchase order numbers will appear as you enter the number into the PO field. A quick-view list of matching purchase order line numbers will appear as you enter the line number into the PO Line field. You must enter the purchase order number in order to change the line number.

·By default, if the referenced purchase order has been entered in the platform all invoice lines must reference the same purchase order.

Note:To reference multiple purchase orders on a single invoice, enable the Allow Multiple PO Reference(s) company preference.

To allow one or more invoice lines to have no purchase order reference, enable the Allow Blank PO Reference(s) company preference.

·If the selected purchase order line includes any coding, it will automatically be populated in the coding fields.

Note:To modify the AFE and Cost Center that is copied from a purchase order, enable the PO Coding Override company preference.

Additional Information

·By default, Leave is in each field indicating that the existing value on selected coding records should not be modified when the coding is applied to that record. Removing the word Leave from a field will result in the existing value being removed from the coding records.

·When modifying general ledger coding, modify or reenter the values in both the Major and Minor fields even if the major is unchanged. The subcode can be left as Leave if adding a new major and minor. If a new major is applied to the line, the current minor and subcodes will be deleted.

·Some fields and data values may result in related fields being automatically applied. For example, an AFE may be associated with a location, so if there is only one location or if one has been set as the default, the location is automatically applied. At this stage, the platform will check that values are valid (if a major.minor.subcode string is applied) and the platform will verify that those codes have been mapped.

·To remove a field from a coding record, delete any text in the field, select the coding record, and click Apply to Selected. For general ledger codes, apply another code to the coding record.

Split Coding

Split coding allows the coder to split the costs associated with a single invoice line across multiple cost objects (two or more AFEs). Coding cannot be split across sites or departments.      

How to Create a Split Allocation for an Invoice

Permission:Invoice Modify

Prerequisite:The invoice status is Submitted or Resubmitted.

Steps:

1.Do one of the following:

§Select Invoice > Dashboard and click one of the dashboard counters.

§Perform a search by invoice number using the Global Search field in the main navigation bar.

§Perform a search with multiple criteria by going to Invoice > Invoice Search.

2.Click the invoice number.

3.Click the Coding tab.

4.Click Split.

 

5.Enter the number of split lines.

6.Select the allocation type.

7.Click Apply.

Note: By default, the amount/percentage will be split evenly across the number of split lines.

8.If necessary, modify the split allocation by doing one or more of the following:

§Click the edit icon in the Amount or % column and adjust the values. Click Submit.

§Click the edit icon to change the coding values in a row or column and click Continue when done. Click Submit.

Note:By default, Leave is in each field, indicating that the current value in the field should stay. Removing the word Leave from a field will result in the existing value being removed from the coding records.

§Select the checkbox(es) of one or more records and click Delete Selected.

Note:When using the Amount allocation type, enter the amount of a single invoice line not including taxes. If the last split was by amount and there is only one line item on the invoice, the Split Coding Wizard will open with the field auto-populated with the pretax amount of the invoice. The splits must total the amount entered. OpenInvoice will accept up to two decimal places for amounts.

When using the Percentage allocation type, the split must total 100%. OpenInvoice will accept up to six decimal places for percentages.

When using the Mixed allocation type, the amount portion is taken from the total and the remainder is split according to the percentages. The percentage splits must total 100%.

9.Click Submit to return to the invoice.

10.Select the checkboxes next to one or more lines.

11.Click Apply to Selected.

Self Assess Taxes

Your company is responsible for ensuring proper payment of taxes based on your use of the product or service delivered. The Self Assess Tax function allows you to create entries in the general ledger for the new tax. The amount coded to the general ledger account of the product/service is increased by the amount of the tax, then offset to an accrual account for the new tax. The total of the invoice does not change. Processing and accounting of refunded taxes is performed outside of the OpenInvoice Platform.

How to Enter Self Assess Taxes

Permission:Invoice Modify

Prerequisite:The invoice must be fully coded and validated as defined by the configured Custom Business Rules.

The invoice status is Submitted or Resubmitted.

Steps:

1.Do one of the following:

§Select Invoice > Dashboard and click one of the dashboard counters.

§Perform a search by invoice number using the Global Search field in the main navigation bar.

§Perform a search with multiple criteria by going to Invoice > Invoice Search.

2.Click the invoice number.

3.Click the Self Assess Tax tab.

Note:The debit account is the product/service account; the credit accounts are the new taxes.

 

4.In the Number of Liability/Clearing/Accrual Accounts (Credit) field, enter the number of accrual accounts (i.e., the number of new taxes being added).

5.Click Next.

6.In the Rate field for the Coding Account (Debit) row, enter the total percentage of the new taxes.

Note:This is the sum of all new tax rates. For example, when adding two new taxes, one with a tax rate of 4% and one with a tax rate of 6%, enter 10 in this field.

7.In the Rate field for each Accrual Account (Credit) row, enter the percentages to be applied towards the new tax accounts.

Note:The sum of the credit percentages must match the debit percentage.

8.Enter the general ledger codes for the new tax accounts. Enter at least the major account.

Note:Only major accounts that do not require a cost object will be returned.

9.Select the checkboxes next to the line items for which the change should apply.

Note:Only non-tax coding records can be selected.

10.Click Apply to Selected.

Note:The platform will generate one debit record (positive value) using the defined default coding account and one credit record (negative value) for each accrual account specified. The net sum of all records generated is zero, so the overall value of the invoice remains the same. 

If the line was split coded, the generated lines are repeated once per split.

11.Make any required changes to the general ledger accounts on the generated records.

12.Click Next or Submit.

Permission:Invoice Admin

Steps:

1.Refer to Split Coding to split code an invoice.

Note:Do not click Submit on the Split Coding Wizard.

2.In the Split Coding Wizard window, click Save Template.

3.Do one of the following:

§Enter a name for your template and select a scope, site, and department.

Note:If Public is selected, choose the site and department that will have access to your template.

Check Coding

The Check Coding function runs the configured coding business rules validations on the fields that have already been applied. It does not check for fields that are required on code verify or on approve. If there is an error, the whole coding record is highlighted. It will display a validation number and an error icon. The validation number can be used to reference the error messages at the top of the page. No coding problems found will be displayed at the top of the page when all coding records have been validated.
