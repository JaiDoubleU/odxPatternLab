Chart of Accounts Configuration

This section provides instructions on how to configure your company’s chart of accounts. Many of OpenInvoice’s time-saving features come from the ability to associate (or map) general ledger codes with various data, allowing buyers to control invoice coding and approvals.

The following topics are applicable to company administrators who oversee configuration of the cost allocation functionalities in OpenInvoice:

·Creating the General Ledger (GL)

·Mapping the General Ledger (GL) to Taxes

Creating the General Ledger (GL)

Each major code is assigned a valid list of minor codes. Optionally, each minor code is assigned a valid list of subcodes. A minor code can be assigned to multiple major codes; a subcode can be assigned to multiple minor codes. Accounting strings identify the various combinations of major, minor, and subcodes. These account strings or mappings are unaffected if changes are made to one of the general ledger account codes or descriptions.

How to Map Minor Accounts to Major Accounts

You can manually map or import your minor account mappings.

To manually map your accounts:

Permission:Corporate Modify

Prerequisite:Coding Level 1 - Major and Coding Level 2 - Minor data has been added under Corporate > Reference Data.

Steps:

1.Select Corporate > COA Setup

2.Click Major Accounts.

Note:By default, all major accounts are listed. Apply a filter to narrow your list. If only part of the criteria is known, an asterisk (*) must be used to denote the unknown part of the criteria.

3.Click the Configure Minor Accounts icon in the Mapped Minor Accounts column.

 

4.Add or remove minor accounts to be mapped by selecting the account name and clicking Add > or < Remove.

Note:To select multiple items at once, hold the Ctrl key and select the items. Alternatively, hold the Shift key to select a range of items.

5.Click Submit.

To import your account mappings:

Permission:Corporate Modify

Prerequisite:Coding Level 1 - Major and Coding Level 2 - Minor data has been added under Corporate > Reference Data.

Steps:

1.Select Corporate > COA Setup

2.Click Major Accounts.

Note:By default, all major accounts are listed. Apply a filter to narrow your list. If only part of the criteria is known, an asterisk (*) must be used to denote the unknown part of the criteria.

3.Click Export to download and save a copy of your existing records in a file.

4.In the file, add your major and minor mappings. For more information about the column headings, refer to Major Minor Mapping Template .

Note:This method can only be used to add mappings. You must use the manual method to delete or modify existing mappings.

5.In OpenInvoice, do one of the following:

§To upload a file saved on your desktop, select File as the import method, click Choose File, navigate to the file on your computer, and click Open.

§To copy the contents of a spreadsheet, select Copy/Paste as the import method, copy the information in your file, and paste it into the box.

6.Click Upload.

Mapping the General Ledger (GL) to Taxes

OpenInvoice automatically codes taxes based on how they are mapped to the general ledger.

How to Map General Ledger Codes to Taxes

You can add a new tax account, modify an existing tax account, or delete a tax account.

To add a new tax account:

Permission:Corporate Modify

Prerequisite:The general ledger must be created by mapping the major, minor, and subcodes.

Steps:

1.Select Corporate > COA Setup.

2.Click Tax Accounts.

3.Click Add Tax Account to create a new mapping.

 

4.Select the Tax Type and Major Account from the drop-down lists. Minor and subcode accounts are optional.

Note:Invoice line items must be coded to at least the major.minor level. If a minor account is not mapped, it must be selected when coding the invoice.

 

5.Click Submit.

To modify an existing tax account:

Permission:Corporate Modify

Prerequisite:The general ledger must be created by mapping the major, minor, and subcodes.

Steps:

1.Select Corporate > COA Setup.

2.Click Tax Accounts.

3.Click the link in the Tax Type column to edit an existing mapping.

 

4.Select the Tax Type and Major Account from the drop-down lists. Minor and subcode accounts are optional.

Note:Invoice line items must be coded to at least the major.minor level. If a minor account is not mapped, it must be selected when coding the invoice.

 

5.Click Submit.

To delete a tax account:

Permission:Corporate Modify

Prerequisite:The general ledger must be created by mapping the major, minor, and subcodes.

Steps:

1.Select Corporate > COA Setup.

2.Click Tax Accounts.

3.Click the Delete icon to remove an existing mapping.

4.Click OK to delete the tax account.
