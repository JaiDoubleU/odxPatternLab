Supplier Registration

Suppliers who are registered in OpenInvoice can create and submit invoices electronically. Supplier registration can be initiated by you or the supplier. Regardless of who initiates the registration process, an on-ramping request will be created.

The supplier can initiate the registration process and enter preliminary company information in one of following ways:

·The suppliers uses your company’s unique OpenInvoice registration link.

·The supplier goes to www.openinvoice.com and does one of the following:

§Select Community > Suppliers and clicks Register As a New Supplier.

§Click Sign In To OpenInvoice, click the Join Now tab, then click the Join Now button, and complete the registration information.

·If the supplier receives email notifications for invoices that your company submits on their behalf, the supplier clicks Register.

Once the new supplier’s preliminary company information is submitted, an on-ramping request is created and your company’s subscribed users (with the Corporate Partnership permission) are notified. The supplier must confirm their email address and proceed with registration by clicking the link in the confirmation email. OpenInvoice Support will verify the supplier’s information before you enable them for transactions.

Note:You can configure the option to have supplier-initiated on-ramping requests assigned to your company or OpenInvoice Support for processing. By default, all new supplier-initiated on-ramping requests are assigned to OpenInvoice Support.

Permission:Corporate Partnership

  2. Click On-Ramp Suppliers in the Tasks section.

The Alert column indicates supplier contacts who have unsubscribed from registration emails.

Supplier Details for On-Ramping

·Registered Company: search field and Search icon used to see if the supplier is already registered in the OpenInvoice Platform. If the supplier name is found in the platform, an on-ramping request is not needed.

·Supplier Number: used to identify supplier sites in all document transfers between your company and OpenInvoice (purchase orders you submit to OpenInvoice, invoice exports from OpenInvoice to you, etc.) Only a supplier number is required to enable a supplier site. The secondary number is not required for supplier site enablement, but can be used to help group a set of supplier sites. For example, each site within a company may have their own supplier numbers, but if they all share the same Remit To address, they may all share the same secondary supplier number.

Note:Supplier numbers can be a maximum of 25 alpha-numeric characters.

The combination of supplier number and secondary supplier number must be unique to the supplier company. More than one site within the supplier company can share the same combination.

·Request Details section: specifies the supplier’s OpenInvoice training needs. Training can be done by OpenInvoice Support or your company.

§Type and Entry Type: OpenInvoice topics that the supplier requires training.

§Training Required: selected by default with the assumption that formal training is expected.

§Ready to Train: indicates that the supplier is registered and ready for training.

§Training Date and Training Time: scheduled training time.

§Current Status: will display New before the invitation is emailed and In Progress after the invitation is emailed, but before supplier self-registration is complete.

§Auto Enable: checked to automatically enable the supplier site (at the address you entered) for transactions once verified by OpenInvoice Support.

Note:A supplier number must be entered to auto enable the supplier.

§Assigned To Buyer: select to make you the owner of the request, allowing you to edit or cancel it if necessary. If not selected, you will not be able to make changes to your request.

Note:OpenInvoice Support can make you the owner of the request later if necessary.

If the request owned by another Corporate Partnership user at your company, you can reassign ownership of the request to yourself.

§Buyer Contact: your name appears by default, but another user with the Corporate Partnership permission can be selected from the drop-down list. Only this user can make changes to the on-ramping request while it is still in progress. This user will receive a notification email when the supplier has finished self-registering.

§OpenInvoice Contact: name of the OpenInvoice Support administrator who worked on this request.

§Other Email: either another email address for your company or an OpenInvoice Support email address.

§Comments: additional information that is visible to your company’s users and OpenInvoice Support.

Viewing Your On-Ramping Request

·Attached Documents section: displays files attached by any Corporate Partnership user in your company

Note:Delete attachments by clicking the Delete icon. Click OK to confirm.

·On-Ramping Journal section: displays on-ramping request activity, user, date, and status

Editing Multiple On-Ramping Requests

·Send Invitation: emails the registration invitation to suppliers

·Buyer Contact: changes ownership of the on-ramping request.

·Ready to Train: notifies the trainer when the supplier is ready for training.

·Auto Enable: selected to automatically enable the supplier site (at the address you entered) for transactions once verified by OpenInvoice Support.

·Status: changes the request statuses to In Progress, Completed, or Cancelled

§In Progress: if the invitation has been sent but supplier setup is not yet complete

§Completed: if the supplier is set up and waiting to be enabled

Note:This status is assigned without verification that the supplier is set up, registered, verified, etc. The status can be changed back to In Progress or Cancelled if necessary.

§Cancelled: if registration is no longer required. Cancelled requests will continue to be displayed in the on-ramping list for up to 30 days to provide opportunity for the request to be changed back to In Progress or Complete.

·Assigned To: selected to transfer ownership to OpenInvoice Support.

Note:You cannot transfer ownership of requests that are not buyer-owned. The option to assign to Buyer is for OpenInvoice Support use only.

How to Modify the Default Assignment of Supplier-Initiated Requests

Permission:Corporate Partnership

Steps:

  1. Select Corporate > Suppliers.

  2. Click On-Ramp Suppliers in the Tasks section.

  3. Click On-Ramping Settings.

  4. Select the Assign unsolicited request to buyer checkbox.

    5. §If the checkbox is selected, all new supplier-initiated on-ramping requests will be assigned to your company.

    §If the checkbox is off, all new supplier-initiated on-ramping requests will be assigned to OpenInvoice Support.

     

  5. Click Back.
