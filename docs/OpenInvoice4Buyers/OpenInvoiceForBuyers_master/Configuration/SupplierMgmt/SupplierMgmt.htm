Supplier Management

Interaction with suppliers will differ depending on whether they are registered OpenInvoice companies or not. For instance, you can process invoices from any type of supplier, but only registered suppliers can submit tickets. This section provides instructions on how to manage supplier companies, both registered and unregistered.

Working with Suppliers

To help increase productivity and reduce data entry time, the most efficient buyer-supplier relationship involves transacting with a registered supplier who has access to the same workflow processes as their buyer; however, some suppliers do not have the capacity to work on the platform so OpenInvoice allows buyer companies to perform some transactions on the supplier’s behalf. Registered suppliers are companies that have been set up and configured for their own users to work in the OpenInvoice Platform. Unregistered suppliers are companies that do not have access to the OpenInvoice Platform, but a company profile is still created for a buyer’s use.

Registered Suppliers

These companies have product and service catalogs that are mapped to the platform’s Supplier Service Index (SSI). Their catalogs, data set, and site information are visible to all buyer companies on the platform. Registered suppliers can send fully electronic tickets and detailed invoices to buyers who have enabled them for transactions. A registered supplier’s invoice expedites the approval process because the invoice can be automatically compared against purchase orders, tickets, and price books.

Before business transactions can occur between a buyer and registered supplier in the OpenInvoice Platform, the buyer must enable the registered supplier’s site(s) for transactions.

Note:Supplier site profile information is visible to your company. Users with the Corporate Partnership permission will receive an email notification if a supplier modifies any of their site profile information.

Unregistered Suppliers

Unregistered supplier information is entered into the platform for one or more of the following reasons:

·Buyers can work on behalf of an unregistered supplier to submit documents such as invoices. This feature allows users from buyer companies to continue working in the OpenInvoice Platform regardless of whether the supplier uses OpenInvoice.

·Basic supplier information, such as site addresses, can be easily referenced on the buyer’s OpenInvoice documents.

·Buyers can automate check payments to unregistered suppliers.

An unregistered supplier’s product and service catalogs are not available on the platform. Communication with unregistered suppliers is external as they do not have access to OpenInvoice.

Unregistered supplier companies can become registered by going through an on-ramping process that includes verification that the company is new to the platform and basic company set up. If using OpenInvoice to pay an unregistered supplier, a user with the Corporate Partnership permission must manually update the necessary information on the supplier’s profile.

Creating the Buyer-Supplier Relationship

Before transacting with and/or referencing a supplier company on your OpenInvoice documents, check to see if your supplier is registered.

If the supplier is registered:

·Select the Supplier Configuration page and enable their site(s) for transactions.

If the supplier does not exist in the platform:

1.Import or upload the supplier’s information to create an unregistered supplier company profile.

2.Create an on-ramping request to begin the registration process for the supplier.

3.Send the unregistered supplier a link to complete the online registration.

4.Select the supplier’s configuration page and enable their site(s) for transactions.

The Supplier Dashboard

The Supplier Dashboard contains two sections: the Suppliers section and the Tasks section.

 

The Suppliers section shows the number of suppliers that have been enabled for transactions and the number of suppliers that are pending setup due to incomplete company information. Click one of the counters to display a list of suppliers.

Note:Suppliers are pending setup if the user adding the company does not have the Corporate Partnership permission or does not have enough supplier information. Invoices can reference suppliers pending setup, but the invoice will be put on hold until setup is complete.

The Tasks section is used to manage suppliers and view supplier lists.

·Search Suppliers: generates a supplier list based on specific search criteria. Leave all fields blank and click Apply Filter to return a list of all suppliers (registered, unregistered, and pending setup).

·Enable Supplier: allows users to select and enable a supplier from a generated list of suppliers that are ready to be enabled. Apply a filter to narrow the list. If criteria is unknown, type an asterisk (*) into one of the fields to return a complete list of suppliers.

·On-Ramp Suppliers: allows users to add or manage on-ramping requests

·Merge Suppliers: allows users to create a merge request for an unregistered supplier. The merge process transfers all existing invoices and purchase orders (including fulfillment history) from the unregistered supplier record to the registered supplier record.

·Export Unregistered Suppliers: allows users to view and/or save a list of unregistered suppliers in a TSV file.

·Import Unregistered Suppliers: allows users to add suppliers or edit existing unregistered supplier company profiles using the exported TSV file.

Supplier Search and Managing Supplier Lists

Supplier searches are performed from either section on the Supplier Dashboard. Clicking a link or counter in one of the sections will direct you to the Supplier Search page where you can apply a filter to search for suppliers. This page also allows users to save search criteria, use and delete saved search criteria, perform actions on a batch of suppliers, export the supplier list page, and customize how the supplier list is viewed.

Note:For instructions on how to perform actions using the Select an Action button, refer to Configuring Smart Coding, Configuring Purchase Order References, Create On-Ramping Requests for Existing Unregistered Suppliers, and Send a Message to Suppliers.

How to Search for Suppliers

Permission:Corporate View

Steps:

  1. Select Corporate > Suppliers.

  2. Do one of the following:

    3. §Click Search Suppliers in the Tasks section.

    §Click a counter in the Suppliers section.

  3. Do one of the following:

§Enter search criteria or leave all fields blank and click Apply Filter.

Note:If only part of the criteria is known, an asterisk (*) must be used to denote the unknown part of the criteria.

To save your search criteria for future searches, click the down arrow on the Apply Filter button and click Save Filter. Only one filter can be saved at a time.

The first 1000 results will be displayed. If you do not see the supplier you are searching for, export and view the entire list of results or refine your search.

§Click the down arrow on the Apply Filter button and click Load Filter to populate the fields and return a list with the most recently saved search criteria.

 

How to Export a List of Suppliers

Permission:Corporate View

Steps:

  1. Select Corporate > Suppliers.

  2. Do one of the following:

    3. §Click Search Suppliers in the Tasks section.

    §Click a counter in the Suppliers section.

  3. Perform a search for suppliers.

  4. Select the checkbox next to all the suppliers you want to export.

    5. Note:Select the checkbox in the table header to auto-select all suppliers.

  5. Click Export Results. A CSV file is exported and downloaded to your system.

How to Customize Your View of the Supplier List Page

Permission:Corporate View

Steps:

  1. Select Corporate > Suppliers.

  2. Do one of the following:

    3. §Click Search Suppliers in the Tasks section.

    §Click a counter in the Suppliers section.

  3. Perform a search for suppliers.

  4. Click Configure Columns to customize your view of the list page.

  5. Do one or more of the following:

    6. §Add or remove column titles from the Configured Columns box by selecting a column title and clicking Add > or < Remove.

    Note:To select multiple column titles at once, hold the Ctrl key and select the columns. Alternatively, hold the Shift key to select a range of columns.

    §Click the arrows to the right of the Configured Columns box to determine the display order of your columns.

    Note:Top-to-bottom order in the Configured Columns box corresponds with left-to-right order on the list page.

    Move a column title one position at a time by clicking the single arrows; use the underlined arrows to move column titles directly to the top or directly to the bottom.

    §Click Default to restore the list page settings to your company default.

     

  6. Click Submit.
