Payment Scheduling

This section includes the payment scheduling terms used in the OpenInvoice platform.

Term

Definition

Banking Day

Any weekday (Monday through Friday) that is not designated as a bank holiday.

Early Pay (EP) Due Date

The latest date on which funds can be transferred to the supplier so that the Early Pay discount can be applied. This is calculated as the Invoice Submission Date plus the Discount Days as defined in the Early Pay program.

Impound Date Adjustment

The number of banking days before the payment date when your company is required to transfer funds to OpenInvoice’s money movement partner.

Payment Base Date

Either the invoice submission date or the invoice date, depending on the invoice’s payment terms.

Payment Date

The date on which the funds are transferred from OpenInvoice’s money movement partner to the supplier. This date must be on a banking day.

Payment Due Date

The date on which the funds must be transferred to the supplier to avoid any penalties. This is the Payment Base Date plus the number of calendar days specified in the payment terms.

Payment Export Date

The date on which the payment details are sent to OpenInvoice’s money movement partner. This occurs one banking day before the Payment Financing Date.

Payment Financing Date (Impound Date)

The date on which OpenInvoice’s money movement partner draws funds from your company’s bank account. This date must be on a banking day.

Preferred Financing Days

Days of the week (Monday through Friday) on which funds are transferred from your company to OpenInvoice’s money movement partner.

Preferred Payment Days

Days of the week (Monday through Friday) on which funds are transferred from OpenInvoice’s money movement partner to the supplier. This is calculated as the Preferred Financing Days plus the Impound Date Adjustment.

Automatic Scheduling of Payments Without Early Pay

If an Early Pay discount does not apply to the approved invoice, OpenInvoice will set the payment date to be as late as possible, while still satisfying the payment terms and Payment Due Date. OpenInvoice will use your company’s default payment terms if supplier-specific payment terms have not been determined.

OpenInvoice calculates the payment date using the following process:

  1. The Payment Due Date is the last banking day on or before the Payment Base Date plus the number of calendar days configured in the payment terms.

  2. The Payment Date is the last Preferred Payment Day that is a banking day, on or before the Payment Due Date.

  3. The Payment Financing Date (Impound Date) is the last Preferred Financing Day that is a banking day, which is before the Payment Date by at least the number banking days defined by the Impound Date Adjustment.

  4. The Payment Export Date is the banking day immediately before the Payment Financing Date (Impound Date).

If same day financing is not enabled and the calculated Payment Export Date is less than one day into the future, it is not possible to satisfy the payment terms based on the current configuration. OpenInvoice will recalculate the Payment Due Date as:

  1. The Payment Export Date is the next banking day.

  2. The Payment Financing Date (Impound Date) is one banking day after the Payment Export Date.

  3. The Payment Due Date is the Payment Financing Date (Impound Date) plus the number of banking days defined by the impound date adjustment.

Note:Changes in payment terms, preferred financing days, impound adjustment, or banking holidays will not automatically reschedule previously scheduled payments. If necessary, the payments must be manually rescheduled by one of your company’s users with the Payment Modify permission.

Automatic Scheduling of Payments with Early Pay

If an Early Pay discount applies to the approved invoice, OpenInvoice will set the payment date to as late as possible while still satisfying the Early Pay discount terms.

OpenInvoice calculates the payment date using the following process:

  1. The Early Pay (EP) Due Date is the last banking day on or before the Invoice Submit Date plus the number of calendar days specified in the Early Pay terms.

  2. The Payment Date is the last Preferred Payment Day that is a banking day, on or before the Early Pay Due Date.

  3. The Payment Financing Date (Impound Date) is the last Preferred Financing Day that is a banking day, which is before the Payment Date by at least the number banking days defined by the Impound Date Adjustment.

  4. The Payment Export Date is the banking day immediately before the Payment Financing Date (Impound Date).

If same day financing is not enabled and the calculated Payment Export Date is less than one day into the future, it is not possible to satisfy the Early Pay terms based on the current configuration. OpenInvoice will recalculate the Payment Due Date as if the invoice is not eligible for an Early Pay discount.

Note:A user from your company can manually reschedule the payment to a non-preferred payment date and take advantage of the Early Pay discount.

Note:Changes in payment terms, preferred financing days, impound adjustment, or banking holidays will not automatically reschedule previously scheduled payments. If necessary, the payments must be manually rescheduled by one of your company’s users with the Payment Modify permission.

Rescheduling Payments

When rescheduling payments, OpenInvoice calculates the Payment Export Date using the following process:

  1. The Payment Financing Date (Impound Date) is the last banking day, on or before the result of subtracting the number of banking days defined by the Impound Date Adjustment from the selected payment date.

  2. The Payment Export Date is the banking day immediately before the Payment Financing Date.
