Configuring Payment Accounts

A payment account consists of six pieces of information: Nickname, Name to Appear on Check, Bank Details, Contacts, Assigned Sites, and Notifications. All items except the notifications are required in order for the payment account to be enabled. OpenInvoice Support must configure the bank details, your payment administrator must configure the contacts and optional notifications, either OpenInvoice Support or your payment administrator must configure the nickname, name to appear on check, and assigned sites.

How to Configure Payment Accounts

Permission:Payment Admin

Steps:

  1. Go to Payment > Payment Accounts.

  2. Do one of the following:

    3. §To create a payment account, click Add Account if you do not see the account listed.

    §To configure or edit an existing account’s details, click Edit.

     

  3. Do one or more of the following:

    4. §Enter or edit the Nickname for the payment account.

    §Enter or edit the Name to Appear on Check printed from this payment account.

    §In the Payment Contacts tab, enter or edit payment contacts.

    Note:The Primary Address is required. Refund checks will be mailed to this address. If necessary, enter a different check address and/or correspondence address. Each address requires an Address line, City, State, and ZIP/Postal Code.

    The number entered in the Phone Number field will be printed on the check stub to allow the supplier to contact your company. The Alternate Phone number is for the individual in charge of payments at your company.

    §In the Assigned Sites tab, assign one or more sites to this payment account.

    Note:A site can only be assigned to a single payment account. Adding a site that is assigned to another payment account will automatically remove the site from the other account.

    §In the Payment Notifications tab, configure your notification schedule and select the role or group of users that will be notified. For more information about payment notifications, refer to “Payment Notifications” on page 8.

    Note:Users will receive one notification for all payments occurring on the same date. A notification will be sent even if there are no payments scheduled for the financing date.

  4. Click Save.

How to Delete a Payment Account

Permission:Payment Admin

Steps:

  1. Go to Payment > Payment Accounts.

  2. If the payment account’s Delete icon is grayed out, unassign all sites from the payment account using the following steps:

  1. Click Edit.

  2. Click the Assigned Sites tab.

  3. Click the left-facing double arrow to remove all sites from the Assigned Sites column.

  4. Click Save.

    5.  

  5. Click Delete in the row of the account you wish to delete.

  6. Click OK to confirm.
