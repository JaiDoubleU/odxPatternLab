Configuring Payment Terms

OpenInvoice uses your configured payment terms to determine when and how to generate and schedule payments for your approved invoices. In addition to your company default payment schedule you are able to define a custom payment schedule for each supplier.

How to Configure Your Company’s Default Payment Terms

Permission:Corporate View and Payment Admin

Steps:

  1. Go to Payment > Payment Terms.

  2. Configure your company’s default payment terms.

    3.  

  3. Click Submit.

Default Payment Term Details

·By default, schedule Payment: defines how OpenInvoice will calculate the date that the invoice will be paid using either the invoice date as entered by the supplier, or the date that the supplier submitted the invoice in OpenInvoice. For example, assume the invoice date is May 1 and the submission date is May 5. If the setting is 15 days after invoice date, the invoice will be paid on May 16. If the setting is 15 days after invoice submission, the invoice will be paid on May 20.

·Finance my Payments every: days of the week that OpenInvoice’s money movement partner can withdraw funds from your bank account in order to finance your payments.

Note:This setting applies to all payment accounts.

·Generate Payments on: determines when OpenInvoice generates a payment record. If Invoice Approval is selected, OpenInvoice will automatically generate a payment record when the invoice status changes to Approved. If Invoice Release For Payment is selected, OpenInvoice will wait until a Release for Payment action is received from your financial system.

·Allow same day financing: determines if OpenInvoice’s money movement partner can withdraw funds from your account on the same day that the payment record is generated. By default, funds will be withdrawn at least one banking day after the payment is generated.

Note:This setting applies to all payment accounts. If this option is enabled, any payments that are financed on the same day the payment is generated will not be included in the payment notifications.

Same day financing is only available if the payment is posted to OpenInvoice’s money movement partner before 12:00 noon Mountain Time.

·Release Payments manually: determines if a payment will be put on hold when the payment is generated. By default, the payment is automatically scheduled and processed without any user intervention. If this option is enabled, every payment must be released by a user before OpenInvoice’s money movement partner will withdraw funds from your account to pay the supplier.

·Apply Credit automatically: determines if available credits from a supplier will be automatically applied to future payments. By default, credits must be manually associated with a payment before the payment amount is reduced. If this option is enabled, the payment amount is automatically reduced by the amount of the available credit.

How to Configure Supplier-Specific Payment Terms

Permission:Corporate View and Payment Admin

Prerequisite:The supplier must be enabled.

Steps:

  1. Go to Corporate > Suppliers.

  2. Do one of the following:

    3. §In the Suppliers portlet, click the Enabled counter.

    §In the Tasks portlet, click Search Suppliers to perform a search.

  3. If necessary, apply a filter to narrow the list of suppliers.

  4. Click the supplier site name.

  5. On the Supplier Configuration page, click Edit under the Payment Terms section.

  6. Select the supplier’s payment schedule configuration.

    7. Note:If modifying existing payment terms, your changes will be effective for newly approved invoices only.

     

  7. Click Submit.

Supplier-Specific Payment Terms

Select from the following payment schedule configurations:

·Company Default: uses your company’s default payment terms.

·Never: the supplier will not be paid through OpenInvoice.

·Custom: defines how OpenInvoice will calculate the date that invoices for this supplier will be paid using either the invoice date as entered by the supplier, or the date that the supplier submitted the invoice in OpenInvoice. For example, assume the invoice date is May 1 and the submission date is May 5. If the setting is 15 days after invoice date, the invoice will be paid on May 16. If the setting is 15 days after invoice submission, the invoice will be paid on May 20.
