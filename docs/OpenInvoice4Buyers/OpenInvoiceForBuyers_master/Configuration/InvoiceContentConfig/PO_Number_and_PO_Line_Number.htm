PO Number and PO Line Number

PO numbers and PO line numbers are created externally from OpenInvoice when your company creates a purchase order.

Relevant Company Preferences

·Allow Blank PO Reference(s)

·Require PO

Note:Invoices submitted from B2B suppliers will be disputed automatically on your company’s behalf if one of the invoice lines does not include a purchase order number.

Image invoices are not affected.

·Prevent Invoice Submission on RED PO Compliance Alerts

Note:This preference has no effect on image invoices or B2B suppliers.

·Require Explicit PO Line

Note:Invoices submitted from B2B suppliers will be disputed automatically on your company’s behalf if one of the invoice lines does not include a purchase order line reference.

External Purchase Order References

By default, if valid purchase orders exist in OpenInvoice for the supplier, the referenced purchase order must be one of the purchase orders in platform. To allow external purchase orders, refer to Configuring Purchase Order References.

How to Make the PO Number and PO Line Number Required Fields

These procedures require the Corporate Admin permissions.

Enable the Require PO Field Preference

Steps:

  1. Select Corporate > Company Preferences.

  2. Click Require PO under the Invoice Content section.

  3. Select the Active checkbox.

    4. Note:This preference has no effect on B2B suppliers.

  4. Click Submit.

Enable the Require Explicit PO Line Field Preference

Steps:

  1. Select Corporate > Company Preferences.

  2. Click Require Explicit PO Line under the Purchase Order section.

  3. Select the Active checkbox.

    4. Note:This preference has no effect on B2B suppliers.

  4. Click Submit.

Configure AFE to Require Either a Cost Center or PO

See the Configure AFE to Require a PO section.

Note:If the Allow Blank PO Reference(s) company preference is enabled then the purchase order required settings are satisfied if at least one invoice line references a purchase order.

Configure Cost Center to Require Either an AFE or PO

See the Configure Cost Centers to Require a PO section.

Note:If the Allow Blank PO Reference(s) company preference is enabled then the purchase order required settings are satisfied if at least one invoice line references a purchase order.

Specify the Invoice Workflow Step That Requires a PO

Steps:

  1. Select Corporate > Workflow Configuration.

  2. Click Invoice Data Fields.

  3. Click the Line Item Detail tab.

  4. Click PO.

  5. Select the appropriate workflow.

    6.  

  6. Click Submit.

Reject Invoices Without a PO

Steps:

  1. Select Corporate > Workflow Configuration.

  2. Click Configure Invoice Workflow Rules.

    3. Note:B2B invoices that are rejected by the invoice workflow rules will be automatically disputed on your behalf.

  3. Import the Invoice Workflow Rules file following the Invoice Workflow Rules Template.

Note:B2B invoices that are rejected by the invoice workflow rules will be automatically disputed on your behalf.

How to Configure Supplier Access

This procedure configures whether a supplier can view or modify POs on an invoice.

Steps:

  1. Select Corporate > Workflow Configuration.

  2. Click Invoice Data Fields.

  3. Click the Line Item Coding tab.

  4. Click PO.

  5. Select the appropriate Supplier Access option from the drop-down list.

  6. Click Submit.

How to Configure Buyer Access

This procedure configures whether users within your company are able to view or modify POs on an invoice.

  1. Select Corporate > Workflow Configuration.

  2. Click Invoice Data Fields.

  3. Click the Line Item Coding tab.

  4. Click PO.

  5. Select the appropriate Buyer Access option from the drop-down list.

  6. Click Submit.
