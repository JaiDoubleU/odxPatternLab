Reports

Reports are requested from various areas of OpenInvoice. Completed reports can be accessed from the Completed Reports portlet or by clicking the Completed Reports icon on the main navigation bar. This section provides an overview of the reports that can be generated in the OpenInvoice Platform.

·Standard Invoice Reports

·Custom Reports

·User Reports

·Price Book Reports

Standard Invoice Reports

This section provides the procedures for generating and viewing standard invoice reports.

How to Request a Standard Invoice Report

Permission:Invoice View

Steps:

1.Select Reports > System Reports.

2.Click a system report.

 

3.Enter the information you want to include in the report.

4.Click Request Report.

Note:The Request Report button may include options for Request Report as Excel or Request Report as CSV. Select the appropriate format for your report.

Invoice Review by Supplier Report

Generates a report providing invoice details for a specified date range that can be filtered by a final approver.

Search Criteria:

·Users can search for invoices with a status of Exported or Approved, or for all invoices. If Exported or Approved is selected, the results can also be filtered by final approver name.

·The user selects a date range of up to 366 days based on the selected status (the report will return invoices exported in the date range if Exported is selected as a status). If All is selected, the Invoice Date will be used.

·Results can be limited to invoices by Unregistered suppliers only.

Results:

·The report returns all invoices matching the selected criteria, grouped by supplier. For each supplier, the report includes:

§One row for every invoice that includes the invoice number, invoice date, submitted date, approval date, final approver, the total amount coded to capital expense, the total amount coded to operational expense, total tax, total invoice amount, and any alerts present on the invoice.

§Summary statistics which includes the total amount of all invoices and the number of invoices.

Custom Reports

Custom reports are configured, scheduled, distributed, and requested from the Custom Reports page.

 

Example of the Custom Reports page.

How to Save a Custom Report Configuration

Permission:Invoice View

Steps:

1.Do one of the following:

§Select Reports > Custom Reports and click the blue New Report icon above the list.

§Select Invoice > Invoice Search.

2.Select the columns, number of results, and how you wish to view the report results. Enter other search criteria as necessary.

3.Select either Summary Report or Detailed Report.

Note:The level of detail for the following report columns depends on the type of report requested. In a detailed report:

Alert: includes whether the document is an image invoice and a written description of the alert type.

Invoice #: displays the invoice’s line numbers each in separate rows.

Invoice Description: includes the product/service code, product/service description, and line description, quantity, unit of measure, rate, line amount, coding amount, and coding amount.

PO: includes the referenced PO line number.

EP Total: includes the Early Pay line total and Early Pay coding total in an invoice list.

Coding: is separated into major, minor, and subcode account number.

Service Date: represents the time period in which the service was provided.

Discount Total: includes the discount amount and discount %.

Tax Total: includes the tax amount and tax type.

Document Id: is a system generated document identifier that is only displayed in reports. It is not available on an invoice list page.

4.Click Save as Report.

Note:There are two Save as Report buttons. Click either button.

5.Enter a report name.

6.Click Save.

How to Edit a Custom Report

Permission:Invoice View

Steps:

1.Select Reports > Custom Reports.

2.Click the report name.

3.Edit your search criteria.

4.Click Save as Report.

5.Modify the report name as necessary.

6.Click Save.

How to Delete a Custom Report

Permission:Invoice View

Steps:

1.Select Reports > Custom Reports.

2.Select the checkbox(es) next to the report name(s) you wish to delete.

3.Click the Delete icon above the list.

How to Configure a Custom Report Schedule

Permission:Invoice View

Note:A maximum of five reports can be scheduled for future generation.

Steps:

1.Select Reports > Custom Reports.

2.Do one of the following:

§To create a custom report schedule, click the add schedule icon in the Schedule column, create a schedule, then click Save.

§To edit a custom report schedule, click the edit schedule icon in the Schedule column, modify the schedule, then click Save.

§To delete a custom report schedule, click the delete icon in the Schedule column, then click OK to confirm.

Note:For monthly recurrence patterns, if 31 is selected as the day of the month, reports will not run in months ending on the 30th.

The recurrence end date is only available if running daily or weekly reports. The end date cannot extend past 366 days of the start date.

How to Distribute a Custom Report

Permission:Invoice View

Note:A report can be distributed to a maximum of 12 recipients.

Steps:

1.Select Reports > Custom Reports.

2.Click the number of recipients in the Recipients column.

3.Add or remove users from the Available Recipients box by selecting a name and clicking Add > or < Remove.

Note:Custom reports can be distributed to any active user in your company. All active users are listed in the Available Recipients box.

To select multiple users, hold the Ctrl key and select the names. Alternatively, you can hold the Shift key to select a range of names.

4.Click Submit.

How to Run a Custom Invoice Report

Permission:Invoice View

Steps:

1.Select Reports > Custom Reports.

2.Do one of the following:

§Click Run Now.

§Click the report name, then click Run Report.

Price Book Reports

The Invoice Spend Report summarizes the total number of invoices and the total invoice amounts allocated to the price book.

Price Book Analysis provides an in-depth look at the detailed coverage and compliance reporting available for price books. The on-demand reporting features provide valuable monitoring and controls for the buyer's strategic sourcing initiatives. Buyers are able to quickly identify how comprehensive the price book coverage is across the entire supply chain. They are also able to monitor individual suppliers to determine if they are compliant with their negotiated price book. In addition to the analysis information provided, reports can be generated and downloaded to the user's computer. Each tab on the Price Book Analysis page can generate two reports: a summary report (Current View), which presents the data as it appears on the page, and a detail report (Details), which includes invoice and line information.

How to Request an Invoice Spend Report

Permission:Price Book View

Steps:

1.Do one of the following:

§Select Price Book > Dashboard and click a counter in one of the portlets.

§Perform a search by Price Book Name using the Global Search field in the main navigation bar.

§Perform a search with multiple criteria by going to Price Book > Search.

2.Click the Price Book Name.

3.Click the Reporting tab.

4.Click Invoice Spend Report.

Note:Totals are calculated separately for each site with at least one invoice allocated to the price book.

Totals are grouped based on the month and/or year in which the invoice received final financial approval. Invoices approved in the current year are grouped by month and invoices approved in any prior year are grouped by year. Invoices that have been submitted but not yet approved are included in the Outstanding column.

Amounts are assumed to be in the price book currency and are totaled as if they all have the same currency. The amounts are the total invoice amount including taxes. Early Pay discounts are ignored.

Volume is the total number of invoices allocated to the price book in the period.

 

How to Request a Price Book Analysis Report

Permission:Price Book Analysis, plus Invoice View for at least one site.

Note:Users will be able to see invoice data for all sites attached to the price book, even if they do not have the Invoice View permission in all sites.

Steps:

1.Select Price Book > Analysis.

2.Enter the date range in the From and To fields. All other filter criteria is optional

Note:The maximum date range is one year based on the invoice's final approval date.

Click YTD to specify a range from January 1 of the current year to the current day.

Click TTM to specify the trailing twelve months from the current date.

Enter the number of previous days from the current date.

3.Click Apply Filter.

4.Click the tab that consists the information you want to report.

Note:The Price Book Coverage tab compares total spend to total spend referencing a price book.

The Price Book Compliance tab illustrates how much of the total spend referencing a price book complies with the price book terms.

5.Select the box(es) next to the suppliers who possess a price book.

6.In the drop-down list next to the Request Report button, select Current View or Detailed.

7.Click Request Report.
