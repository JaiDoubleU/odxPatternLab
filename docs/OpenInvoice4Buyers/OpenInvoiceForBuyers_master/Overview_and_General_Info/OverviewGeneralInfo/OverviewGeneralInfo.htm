Overview and General User Information

OpenInvoice transforms the way organizations manage accounts payable and procurement. Whether you are looking for a simple solution with basic imaging-and-workflow capabilities or a fully-integrated electronic invoicing application tightly linked to existing internal accounting systems and supplier applications, OpenInvoice can move your procure-to-pay (P2P) processes into the 21st Century.

OpenInvoice provides the following functionality:

·OpenTicket: enables you and your suppliers to eliminate the paper, simplify processes, and significantly reduce administrative load. With a paperless field ticket process your field service professionals can focus on their jobs in the field, not at their desks; accounting professionals can easily identify exceptions and focus on maintaining cost-control; and operational decision makers can plan for the future with real-time spend visibility.

·Purchase Orders: offers configurable purchase order submission functionality, accepts approved POs from client’s procurement systems and routes them, along with any attachments, to the appropriate supplier electronically. Suppliers are immediately notified about issued POs and can access them from within OpenInvoice.

·Electronic Invoicing: supports electronic invoicing in a number of ways - from hands-free business-to-business (B2B) integration for high volume suppliers to flipping purchase orders or creating invoices in the portal for low volume suppliers - and allows your suppliers to pick and choose the method that works best for them. OpenInvoice even supports suppliers that continue to send paper invoices through outsourced scanning and indexing.

·Approval workflow: offers comprehensive approval workflow functionality that helps ensure that all invoices are routed to the appropriate users and approved in a timely manner. OpenInvoice puts you in complete control of the process, allowing you to configure the approval criteria to support your unique business rules and requirements.

·Price Compliance: you can easily ensure that invoices are processed according to negotiated price books. The system automatically checks every invoice, at the line item level, and instantly determines whether a price is correct or off-price book. Discrepancies are flagged and suppliers alerted immediately before the invoice is even submitted.

·Discount Management: electronic invoicing and automated workflow capabilities reduce processing cycles significantly, allowing you to capture more available discounts. OpenInvoice’s dynamic discounting capabilities enable you to dynamically change standard terms to accelerate payments using a sliding discount scale, driving incremental discounts.

·Payment Processing: makes payment automation simple - offering the flexibility of paying suppliers electronically or via check - reducing costs, increasing controls and lowering barriers for suppliers. OpenInvoice’s payment functionality is tightly integrated with the invoice automation application, offering an end-to-end solution from invoice to payment from a single platform.

Working with Windows and Microsoft Office

This section provides information for viewing TSV files and entering numbers in an Excel spreadsheet to ensure the files are compliant with the OpenInvoice application.

Configuring Windows to View TSV Files

Before OpenInvoice reports and exported files can be viewed, your computer must be configured to open Tab Separated Value (TSV) files with a spreadsheet application such as Microsoft Excel.

To configure Windows to automatically open TSV files in Excel:

1.In Windows Explorer, navigate to the directory where the file is saved.

2.Right-click the file.

3.Select Open With > Choose Program and select Microsoft Excel from the list of programs.

4.Select the Always use the selected program to open this kind of file checkbox.

5.Click OK.

Entering Numbers in Excel

If data is being entered into an Excel spreadsheet for use in the OpenInvoice Platform, ensure your numbers are saved in the correct format. By default, Excel will automatically change longer numbers into scientific format which is not recognized by the OpenInvoice Platform.

·Numbers longer than 11 digits will be converted into scientific format. (This does not apply to digits combined with letters and/or symbols.)

·Any numbers after the 15th digit will be displayed as zeros in scientific format.

·A period in a number can be misinterpreted as a decimal and digits following the period may be removed.

To prevent automatic formatting in Excel:

1.Right-click the column in which you are entering your numbers.

2.Select Format Cells.

3.In the Format Cells window, click the Number tab.

4.Select Text.

5.Click OK.

6.Enter or re-enter the number (if it has already been converted to scientific format).

Note:Disregard the Number Stored as Text warning if it appears.

Account Setup

Before using the OpenInvoice platform for the first time you must complete the registration process.

User Registration

You will receive a registration email to set up your account. To complete the registration process, click the link in the registration email. Follow the instructions that include entering your username (provided by OpenInvoice Support or your company administrator), creating a password, agreeing to the terms of use, defining your security questions and answers, and confirming your profile.

Note:If you enter an incorrect username or you do not complete registration within 14 days of receipt of the registration email, you must contact your administrator to have the registration email resent.

Account Lockout

If you enter an incorrect username and password after the defined number of attempts your account will be locked. Your company administrator configures the allowable number of attempts, as well as the amount of time that must pass before your account is unlocked. This lockout time can be specified as indefinite, 5, 10, 15, or 30 minutes. If the lockout time is indefinite, a user with the Membership Modify permissions can manually unlock your account.

Sessions

If multiple users share a computer, each user must sign in from a different browser session. Opening a new tab within a browser is still considered as a single session. Although you may be able to sign in on the new tab, any changes made in the new tab will be reflected in the old tab. To begin a new session, consult your browser’s instructions.

Session Time Out

Your session will automatically time out after 30 minutes of inactivity. Five minutes before your session times out, a pop-up window will appear to provide you with the option of extending your session. If you do not respond, any unsaved data will be lost and you will be directed to the sign in page.

Keep Me Signed In (Optional)

This feature is a secure way to allow users to repeatedly access OpenInvoice without having to reenter their username and password. Settings for this feature are configured by users with the Corporate Admin permission.

Note:This feature is device and browser specific. Your company administrators may limit the device on which you can use this feature. To use this feature across various devices and browsers, you must select the Keep me signed in checkbox on each device and in each browser you use.

Expired Passwords

Your company administrator configures the number of days before your password will automatically expire. One week before your password expires, you will receive a warning each time you sign in. After your password has expired, you can still sign in, but you will be prompted to change it immediately. If you do not change your password within 180 days of password expiration, your account will become suspended. To access your suspended account, contact OpenInvoice Support or your company administrator to have your account reactivated.

Sign Out

Before closing the browser window, remember to click Log Out to end the session. If you do not sign out, your session will keep running until it times out. If you decide to sign in again before your session has timed out, you will receive a message stating that you are already signed in. If you are already signed in, click Continue to terminate the old session and sign in to the new session.

Summary Tab

This tab displays a summary of your user profile including organization roles, authorization level history, delegation history, and licenses. The other tabs on this page allow you to configure your profile.

Profile Tab

This tab allows you to configure your general user information.

·Active: grants the user access to OpenInvoice. When deselected, the user is no longer able to access OpenInvoice; however, their existing password and security questions are maintained in case they are reactivated.

·Self-Manage: allows the user to assign themselves roles, permissions, and authorization levels (if the user is also assigned the Membership Manage Auth permission). This checkbox is only visible to users who have been granted the Self-Manage attribute. The user can disable this attribute on their profile; however, if you want to enable the Self-Manage functionality, then another user with Self-Manage permissions must select this checkbox for your profile.

Note:The Membership Modify permission is required to modify the Employee # and Job Title fields.

Note:If your email address was updated, a confirmation email will be sent to the old email address.

Preferences Tab

The Preferences tab allows you to subscribe to receiving notifications using Message Center and email and also update your general preferences. In the Message Center Preferences some subscriptions are required, so the Subscribe checkbox may be grayed out.

Message Center Preferences

Message Center preferences apply to the following notification types:

·Disputed Invoice (Invoice Modify IM permission only): notification when an invoice you indexed and submitted has been disputed by another user.

·Completed Report: notification when your requested report is available for viewing.

·New Supplier Request (Corporate Partnership permission only): notification when a supplier has used your company’s unique registration link or when your enabled supplier’s company name and tax ID have changed. If the supplier has undergone a tax ID change, the notification will request you enable the supplier under the new company name.

·Integration Services (Corporate Integration permission only): notification of important events and disruptions in your integration services. Messages will be displayed in your Message Center and in the Integration Services Notification portlet on the Integration Services page.

·Security Warning: notification when your email address has been changed either by you or an administrator. The notification is sent to your original email.

Note:This preference is enabled for all users and cannot be disabled.

·User Email Change (Membership Manage Auth, Membership Modify and Membership Admin only): notification when any user’s email address has changed or another administrator who is subscribed to this preference has been unsubscribed.

·Cancelled Ticket: users can select if they want to be notified when suppliers cancel a field ticket.

·Oildex Communications: notifications from Oildex.

Other Preferences

·Preferred Language: displays your preferred language.

·Receive Messages on changes to registered suppliers (Corporate Partnership permission only): notification when a registered supplier modifies their company name, tax number, address, or pay-to name.

·Receive Outage Notifications: notification when the OpenInvoice Platform will be undergoing maintenance.

·Receive Messages by email: sends a copy of your notifications to your email address.

Note:This preference is not applicable to the Disputed Invoice, Completed Report, New Supplier Request, and Integration Services notifications.

·Receive email on invoices requiring my action (Invoice Code and Invoice Approve permissions only): notifications about invoices requiring your action. Notifications are sent on a daily basis until the action has been performed, delegated, or forwarded to someone else.

Note:If you have the Invoice Code permission, you will receive notifications for any invoices displayed as Pending Coding Review or Pending Coding Verification in your Invoice Dashboard.

Note:If you have an authorization level, you will receive notifications for any invoices displayed as Pending Financial Approval.

Note:This setting has no effect on stalled document notifications.

·Only include invoices received within last 24 hours: limits notifications about invoices requiring your action to only those invoices received within the last 24 hours (including weekends and statutory holidays).

Note:This option is only available if you have selected the Receive email on invoices requiring my action checkbox.

·Receive email after Amend action on invoices I processed: sends you an email if another user modifies the coding on an invoice that you have approved or coded.

Global Search

To locate a price book, field ticket, purchase order, or invoice from any page, the Global Search field is located on the main navigation bar. Select a document type and enter all or part of the document number. An asterisk (*) must be used to denote the unknown part of the document number. Click the Search icon to reveal a matching list of documents.

As you type an invoice number, up to 20 matching invoices are displayed in a quick-view list. The list is based on the most recent invoice dates, then sorted by supplier name. The invoice number, supplier name, submission date, currency, total, and status of the invoice are also displayed. Selecting an invoice from the quick-view list will open a read-only version in a new window. The quick-view list does not appear for price books or field tickets.
