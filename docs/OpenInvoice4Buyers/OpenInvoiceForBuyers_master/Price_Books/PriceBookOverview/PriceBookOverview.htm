Price Books

Price books are negotiated between buyers and suppliers outside of the OpenInvoice Platform, and the price book document is created by the buyer and then the pricing is uploaded by the buyer (or supplier if Supplier Collaboration is turned on).This section provides an overview of how to view and use price books in OpenInvoice.

Note:Buyers have the ability to allow their suppliers collaboration access to their price books. Suppliers can view all price books without collaboration access.

Price books provide:

·consistent descriptions on products and services

·control and tracking of negotiated and expired rates

·compliance alerts that advise buyers and suppliers when an invoice is or is not in compliance

Price books created by a buyer are only accessible to that specific buyer and their suppliers. Other companies cannot see or access the price book.

Price Book Collaboration

Buyers can easily collaborate with their suppliers on Price Books by allowing suppliers the ability to edit the price book in OpenInvoice. When a price book is being reviewed and in Requested for Edit mode, by either the buyer or supplier, the price book is locked to all other users and a message displays indicating the price book is locked.

Price Book Dashboard

The Price Book Dashboard is accessed by going to Price Book > Dashboard. The dashboard consists of two portlets:

·The Price Books Requiring My Actions portlet displays counts and provides direct access to the price books you are responsible for processing.

·The Price Books portlet displays counts and provides direct access to the price books you have been granted permission to view.

 

Price Books Requiring My Actions Portlet

This portlet only includes the price books that the user is able to process based on their assigned permissions and the price book status.

·Pending Change – Requested: price books with a working status of Requested for which the user has the Price Book Modify permission.

·Pending Change – Disputed: price books with a working status of Disputed for which the user has the Price Book Modify permission.

·Pending Approval – Submitted: price books with a working status of Submitted for which the user has the Price Book Approve permission.

·Pending Approval – Re-submitted: price books with a working status of Resubmitted for which the user has the Price Book Approve permission.

Price Book Portlet

Each row in this portlet represents a price book status. The portlet only includes price books that the user is able to view.

Note:It is possible for a price book to be included in more than one category.

·Active: price books that have been approved at least once and the current date is between the price book’s active and expiry dates.

·Approved: price books that have been approved at least once and the current date is before the price book’s active date.

·In Progress (new price books): price books that have never been approved and have a working status of New, Requested, Submitted, Disputed, or Resubmitted.

·In Progress (amendments): price books that have been approved at least once and have a working status of Requested, Submitted, Disputed, or Resubmitted for which the user has view permission.

·Inactive: price books that are in a Cancelled, Terminated or Expired status.

Note:Price books will be automatically removed from this category after the time period defined by the preference Days to Display Inactive/Expired Price Books.

·Expiring in X Days: price books that are active and the price book’s expiry date is within the number of days specified in the company preference Days to Notify in Advance of Price Book Expiry.

Price Book Search

Price book searches are performed by specifying multiple search criteria or by entering the price book name into the Global Search field. The resulting list displays the price books based on current amendments. Price book search results display the approved version of the pricing. If the price book is being amended, only the last approved prices are available for invoices or field tickets.

How to Search Using Global Search

Permission:Price Book View

Steps:

1.In the main navigation bar, click the down arrow in the Global Search field and select Price Book Name.

 

2.Enter all or part of the Price Book Name.

Note:If only part of the price book name is known, an asterisk (*) must be used to denote the unknown part of the price book name.

3.Click the Search icon

4.Select a Price Book Name from the list.

How to Search Using Price Book Search

Permission:Price Book View

Steps:

1.Select Price Book > Search.

2.Enter your search criteria.

Note:If only part of the Supplier Name or User are known, an asterisk (*) must be used to denote the unknown part of the name. Click the Search icon, select a supplier or user, and click Submit. Alternatively, leave the fields blank and click Submit to return a list of all price books.

Click Reset to clear all search fields.

 

3.Click Submit.

Note:By default, price books are sorted by Price Book Name. Sort by clicking any column header.

 

 

Note:If the Supplier Collaboration setting is “No,” then the supplier will have view-only access to the price books.

Price Book Structure

A price book consists of the Summary, Price Entries, Contacts, and Documents.

Note:The price book contacts and documents sections are optional.

 

Supplementary Information

Expanding this section will display the Price Book Journal and messages related to the price book. The Price Book Journal displays historical workflow actions that have been performed against the price book as well as any comments that have been entered. Comments are restricted to your company by default. The journal entries are displayed with the most recent entry at the top and the oldest at the bottom.

Note:Click Approved to open a read-only historical version of the price book. To return to the current price book, click View Current Version at the top of the Price Book Journal.

Expand the Approved line to display the workflow actions leading up to the approval.

Summary

This tab displays the price book header information, including the price book name, agreement number, associated supplier name, price book status, currency, price book description, project information, and Price Book values.

From this tab, you can also configure the sites (buyer and supplier) that are applicable to the price book.

Price Entries

This tab displays the product and service pricing agreed upon between the buyer and supplier.

 

From this tab, you can make changes to your pricing records and/or the price book.

Early Pay

The Early Pay tab is read-only and displays any existing Early Pay terms that were configured prior to the release of OpenInvoice 14.2.

Early Pay programs are replacing the existing Early Pay terms configured in price books. Existing Early Pay price books with a supplier will continue to be used until you assign an Early Pay program to that supplier. Once an Early Pay program is assigned to a supplier all price books with that supplier that include Early Pay terms are disabled.

 

Reporting

Available reports for the price book are listed on this tab. The reports are displayed by name and description.

Documents

Comments and attached documents applicable to the price book are displayed.

Contacts

Contacts from your company, the supplier company, and/or a third-party are listed in this tab. The buyer and supplier contacts can be registered or unregistered OpenInvoice users. Third-party contacts are unregistered.

Administration

You can assign roles and permissions for this price book in this tab.

Price Book Notifications

There are two types of notifications, Subscribed Notifications and Standard Notifications.

Subscribed Notifications

Note:If a user has access to multiple price books and the same notification condition occurs on other price books, the user will only receive one email notification for all price books.

By default, notifications are disabled.

All users assigned to the selected role will receive notifications if the role is subscribed to any of the following:

·Action Required: notifies users in real-time when they are required to act on their assigned price books. Notifications are sent to price book modifiers when the price book working status becomes Requested or Disputed. Notifications are sent to price book approvers when the price book working status becomes Submitted or Resubmitted.

·Major Event: notifies users, based on a defined schedule, when their assigned price books are disputed, approved, activated, expired, cancelled, or terminated.

·Price Book Change: notifies users, based on a defined schedule, if the price book is activated or modified when the price book working status is Requested.

·Spend Threshold: notifies users, based on a defined schedule, when the total spend for an Active or Expired price book exceeds 85% of the Estimated Price Book Value.

Standard Notifications

All users within roles that are assigned to the price book will receive the following standard notifications. These notifications are not listed in the Notifications section for a price book. A role cannot unsubscribe from these notifications.

·Price Book Expiry: notifies price book administrators when a price book is about to expire. The notification will be sent twice. One notification is sent based on the company preference Days to Notify In Advance of Price Book Expiry and the other notification is sent one day prior to the price book expiry date.

·Supplier Product/Service Catalog Change: notifies price book administrators and modifiers when a product/service description in the price book is modified in the supplier’s catalog.

Price Book Compliance Alerts

Price Book compliance alerts notify the buyer and supplier when an invoice or ticket is not in compliance with the price book it references. Alerts are categorized by severity: red, yellow, or blue. By default, red is the most severe, blue is the second most severe, and yellow is the least severe. The buyer has the option to enable an Oildex-controlled preference that will modify alert severity so that yellow takes priority over blue; if the preference is enabled, red is most severe, yellow is second most severe, and blue is least severe. An invoice/ticket that is compliant with the referenced price book will have a green alert. Click the alert icon to display a message with details about the alert condition.

Determining Compliance

OpenInvoice compares the information entered on a ticket or invoice’s line items to find the correct pricing record in the price book.

Note:The preferred unit of measure is used for price book reconciliation. The invoice’s unit of measure is translated to the preferred unit of measure. If the invoice’s unit of measure does not exactly match the unit of measure in the price book, then a “Off Price Book” alert displays.

Price records may be referenced on a ticket or invoice lines if:

·The Service Date (or Service Date From if using a range) overlaps the price record’s Date From range.

·The  Quantity or the total Quantity is greater than or equal to the price record’s quantity threshold.

·The Unit matches the price record’s unit of measure, or the price record does not have a unit of measure specified.

·The Qualifier matches the price record’s qualifier.

·The Adjusted Rate in the pricing record matches the Adjusted Rate. To calculate the Adjusted Rate, subtract the discount from the rate. For a price range, the ticket or invoice line’s Adjusted Rate must be between the Min Adjusted Rate and Max Adjusted Rate.

·The preferred unit of measure is used for price book reconciliation. If the invoice’s unit of measure does not match the preferred unit of measure, the invoice’s unit of measure will be used for price book reconciliation.
