Price Book Approval Workflow

The Price Book Approval workflow provides the tools necessary to modify, review, and approve material changes to a price book. Each revision of the price book is archived and historical versions can be viewed through the Price Book Journal. All price books must go through the approval workflow at least once. Once the price book has been approved, any changes to the Price Book or the active and expiry dates must go through the approval workflow.

Price Book Workflow

Price books are created and amended in OpenInvoice following the process below:

1.A price book administrator creates a price book, configures its sites, adds roles, then places it into edit mode.

2.A price book modifier enters price book details including the active and expiry dates, contacts, and documents. After all details have been entered, the price book modifier submits the price book for approval.

3.A price book approver reviews the price book, then approves or disputes. If disputed, the price book is sent back to the modifier for revision and resubmission for approval.

4.Once the price book is approved, OpenInvoice activates the price book on the specified active date.

5.Once the price book is activated, suppliers can create invoices and/or field tickets referencing the price book.

Price Book Workflow Optimization

OpenInvoice optimizes price book workflow for users who have been granted multiple permissions:

·A user who is both price book administrator and price book modifier can create and edit a price book in a single step.

·A user who is both price book modifier and price book approver can submit and approve a price book in a single step.

·If a price book does not have a price book approver assigned, OpenInvoice will automatically approve the price book after it is submitted by a price book modifier.

Price Book Status

The price book status determines when a price book is available for referencing on a ticket or invoice.

Note:To reference a price book, the service date must be between the price book’s active and expiry dates.

Status

Available for Reference

Description

New

 

A new price book that has not been requested for edit.

Approved

 

Price book has been reviewed and approved.

If no revision is in progress, the price book and price book header are read-only and cannot be modified.

Active

X

Approved price book that is automatically activated once the current date is between the price book’s active and expiry dates. You are viewing the price book on a date within the price book’s active and expiry dates.

If no revision is in progress, the price book and price book header are read-only and cannot be modified.

Expired

X

Active price book has expired. You are viewing the price book on a date that is past the expiry date.

Expired price book can be renewed by editing the expiry date.

If no revision is in progress, the price book and price book header are read-only and cannot be modified.

Terminated

X

Active price book has been terminated by a price book administrator.

Entire price book is read-only and cannot be modified.

Terminated price books can be reactivated using the amendment process (selecting Request for Edit).

Cancelled

 

A price book that has been cancelled.

Proposed Pricing Status

A price book’s proposed pricing status does not affect whether the price book can be referenced on an invoice or field ticket. This status tracks the status of any proposed pricing request.

Status

Description

Requested

The pricing has been requested.

Submitted

The price book pricing has been completed and the version is now waiting for review and approval.

Re-Submitted

The price book pricing has been updated and re-submitted to the approver.

Disputed

The price book pricing has been reviewed and disputed by the price book approver.

blank

The request for price book pricing has been cancelled, or an amendment has not been initiated.

Creating a Price Book

This section contains the procedure for creating a price book.

How to Create a Price Book

A buyer must create the price book. Suppliers can modify a price book if the Supplier Collaboration setting is On.

Permission:Price Book Admin (Site or Corpor   ate)

Steps:

1.Select Price Book > Create.

 

2.Select a Supplier Name.

Note:Price books can only be set up for registered suppliers.

3.Enter a Price Book Name and Price Book Description.

4.Select the Currency.

5.(Optional) If you want your supplier to collaborate on your price book:

1.Select On for Supplier Collaboration.

2.Select Yes for Send Notification To Supplier if you want your supplier to be notified that the price book has been created.

6.(Optional) Enter additional field information.

7.Click Create or Create and Edit.

Note:The Create and Edit button is available only if you have the Price Book Modify and Price Book Admin permissions.

Price Book Details

·Price Book Name: must be unique for a supplier.

Note:The Price Book Name can be reused for a different supplier.

·Agreement Number: can be reused for different price books for the same supplier, but cannot be the same for different suppliers.

Important!The combination of a Price Book Name and Agreement Number must be unique.

·Currency: a price book must have a single currency used for all rates and amounts on the price book.

·Estimated Value: 

§Price Book: estimated total spend of all invoices referencing this price book. The spend threshold notification will be sent when the total of all approved invoices referencing the price book reaches 85% of this value.

§Minimum and Maximum: lower and upper limits of the negotiated value of the price book. These values are for reference only.

·Auto Assign Roles: OpenInvoice will assign all roles that have one of the Price Book View, Price Book Modify, or Price Book Approve permissions.

Note:If the price book is created by an administrator with the Price Book Admin (Site) permission only the price book roles in that site will be added.

If the price book is created by an administrator with the Price Book Admin (Corporate) permission all price book roles in the company will be added.

If this setting is not selected, price book administrators will need to manually assign roles before the price book can go through the editing process.

·Supplier Collaboration: allows suppliers to provide updates to price books.

·Send Notification To Supplier: if set to Yes, then an email will be sent to the supplier when the price book is created.

How to Add a Single Product or Service to a Price Book

Permission:Price Book Modify

Note:A user with Price Book Modify permissions can only modify pricing if the role with the Price Book Modify permissions is assigned to the price book.

Prerequisite:A price book administrator has initiated the Price Book Approval workflow.The price book status must be New or Active.

Steps:

1.Do one of the following:

§Select Price Book > Dashboard and click a counter in one of the portlets.

§Perform a search by Price Book Name using the Global Search field in the main navigation bar.

§Perform a search with multiple criteria by going to Price Book > Search.

2.Click the Price Book Name.

3.Click the Price Entries tab.

Note:By default, all pricing records for all products and services are listed. Apply a filter to narrow the list. Enter all or part of the product/service and click Apply Filter.

 

4.Click Request for Edit to update the price book.

Note:The price book is now locked by the user.

5.Click Add Product/Service to add a new product/service to the price book.

6.Enter all or part of a description in the Select Product/Service field and click the Search icon. Use an asterisk (*) to denote the unknown part of the description.

 

7.Select the product/service from the list then click Submit.

 

8.(Optional) Enter the Purchase Category, Item Master, and/or Detail Comments.

Note:The Purchase Category is a buyer-managed list and supports a code and description. The field supports type-ahead entries and will display matches as you enter text. You can type *** to display the first 10 Purchase Categories.

9.Do one or more of the following:

§If a blue circle alert appears, the supplier has modified the product/service in their catalog. Click Reconcile Price Book to apply the supplier’s modification to the price book.

Note:After reconciling, the alert and Reconcile Price Book button will disappear.

§Click Add Price Item to add a blank pricing record. The record is added in edit mode. Click Continue when done editing the pricing record.

§Click the edit row or edit column icon to put a row or column in edit mode. Click Continue when done editing the row or column.

§Click the Delete icon on a pricing record to remove that record.

§Click Delete All Pricing to remove all pricing records.

10.Click Submit.

Modifying a Price Book Using File Upload

This section provides the procedure for modifying a price book using a spreadsheet.

Important!The export/import file is an XLSX file. We have aligned the column headers with those you see when you download a supplier’s catalog. If you have templates that you maintain outside of OpenInvoice, you will need to update them and then save the file in XLSX format.

How to Import and Export the Price Book

Permission:Price Book Modify in a role assigned to a price book

Prerequisite:In order to import a price book a price book administrator must have initiated the Price Book Approval workflow.The price book status must be New or Active.

Steps:

1.Do one of the following:

§Select Price Book > Dashboard and click a counter in one of the portlets.

§Perform a search by Price Book Name using the Global Search field in the main navigation bar.

§Perform a search with multiple criteria by going to Price Book > Search.

2.Click the Price Book Name.

3.Click the Price Entries tab.

 

4.Do one of the following:

§Click Export Price Book to download and save the existing price book products and services with their associated pricing records. Use this file to modify existing price book items.

§Click Export Catalog to download and save the existing price book products and services with their associated pricing records as well as every product/service in the supplier’s catalog that is not part of the price book. Use this file to modify existing price book items and/or add any additional products and services from the supplier’s catalog to the price book.

5.In the exported file, modify the pricing records. For more information about the column headings, refer to Price Book Template or Price Book/Supplier Catalog Template .

Note:All existing pricing records must be in the final XLSX file or they will be deleted when the file is imported.

OpenInvoice automatically determines if a record needs to be added or modified based on the data of the records in the XLSX file.

Leave the Action column blank for records that are added or modified.

Enter Delete in the Action to remove the pricing record from the price book.

Do not modify or delete values in the Row#(DO NOT MODIFY/DELETE) column.

6.Save your updated file in XLSX format.

7.In OpenInvoice, click Import/Update on the Price Entries tab of the price book to be modified.

 

8.Do one of the following:

§To upload a file saved on your desktop, select File as the import method, click Choose File, navigate to the file on your computer, and click Open.

§To copy the contents of a spreadsheet, select Copy/Paste as the import method, copy all columns and rows with data in your file, and paste it into the box.

9.Click Upload.

10.In the Price Book Import Staging Area, review the proposed changes to the price book.

§Expand the Legend for a description of the review symbols.

 

§Expand the rows to view previous values.

11.Select the changes to apply.

12.Click Apply Changes.

Removing Early Pay Terms on Price Books

Early Pay programs are replacing the existing Early Pay terms configured in price books. Existing Early Pay price books with a supplier will continue to be used until you assign an Early Pay program to that supplier. Once an Early Pay program is assigned to a supplier all price books with that supplier that include Early Pay terms are disabled. Refer to Configuring Early Pay Programs.
