Processing Payments through OpenInvoice

A payment can only be processed through OpenInvoice if the:

·buyer and supplier addresses on the invoice are in the United States of America.

·invoice currency is USD.

·invoice amount is less than $100 million.

Processing Credits through OpenInvoice

A credit is available to apply to upcoming payments once your company processes a credit invoice in OpenInvoice. Based on your company’s configuration, OpenInvoice may automatically apply the credit to the next scheduled payment or a user from your company may have to manually apply it. If you settle the credit amount with your supplier outside of OpenInvoice, a user from your company will mark the credit as Settled Externally.

Returned Items

When you update an unregistered supplier’s site configuration to receive ACH payments, OpenInvoice’s money movement partner will confirm the banking details by sending a prenote to the supplier’s bank. If the banking details are invalid, the bank will return the prenote.

An ACH payment will be returned by the bank if the supplier’s banking details no longer match the information that the bank has on file.

If a prenote or an ACH payment is returned, OpenInvoice will notify all users in the payment notification role and compare the banking details of the returned item with the supplier site’s configuration. If the banking details in the returned item matches the supplier site’s configuration, OpenInvoice will automatically change the payment method to check. If the banking details in the returned item are different than the supplier site’s configuration, OpenInvoice will assume you have already corrected the error.
