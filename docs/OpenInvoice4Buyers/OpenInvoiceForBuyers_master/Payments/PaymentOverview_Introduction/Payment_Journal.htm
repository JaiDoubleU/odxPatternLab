Payment Journal

The Payment Journal displays actions that have been performed against the payment. Journal entries are displayed with the most recent entry at the top and the oldest at the bottom.

Workflow Actions Visible in the Payment Journal

Workflow actions and comments associated with the workflow actions are displayed in the Payment Journal. Workflow actions are also displayed in the Invoice Journal.

Actions Visible on Debits

The following actions apply for both check and ACH payments.

·Scheduled: OpenInvoice has scheduled the payment based on your company’s configuration and the invoice’s eligibility for an Early Pay discount.

·Re-scheduled: a user from your company changed the payment date.

·Held: the payment was either removed from the workflow by OpenInvoice based on your company’s configuration or a user from your company manually removed the payment from the workflow. The payment will not be processed until it is released.

·Released: a user from your company released a previously held payment and it has returned to the workflow.

·Posted: payment details were sent to the OpenInvoice money movement partner for processing.

·Cancelled: a user from your company requested a stop payment before OpenInvoice’s money movement partner completed processing the payment.

·Re-submitted: a user from your company restarted the payment process for an invoice with a previously cancelled or stopped payment.

·Terminated: a user from your company marked the payment as end-of-life and the invoice will not be paid through OpenInvoice.

Note:The associated invoice can be returned to workflow, adjusted, and once approved OpenInvoice will generate a new payment.

·Credit Applied: the original payment amount was reduced by applying a credit. The action comment identifies the amount of credit applied and the credit invoice that was used. Any amount remaining will be paid on the scheduled payment date.

·Paid: either the entire amount of the invoice was covered by applying a credit or an ACH payment was processed and funds have been transferred. The action comment indicates if the invoice was paid by applying a credit or paid by ACH.

Note:Invoices paid by check do not have a paid action.

The following actions apply to ACH payments only.

·ACH Payment Returned: funds cannot be transferred to the supplier’s bank account due to a configuration error.

The following actions apply to check payments only.

·Check Issued: OpenInvoice’s money movement partner issued and printed a check for the payment.

·Check Voided: a user from your company requested a stop payment before the check was mailed or the check was returned by the post office as undeliverable. Once the check is voided, the payment is available for a user from your company to reschedule or terminate.

·Check Stop Requested: a user from your company requested a stop payment after the check was mailed. OpenInvoice Support will forward the stop request to the bank; however, a stop cannot be guaranteed.

·Check Stop Denied: the bank indicated that your company’s stop check request was not successful.

·Check Stopped: the bank has indicated that your company’s stop check request was successful.

·Check Settled: the supplier has cashed the check and the funds have been transferred.

Actions Visible on Credits

The following actions only apply to credit invoices.

·Credit Applied: the credit was used to reduce the payment amount of a debit invoice. The action comment identifies the amount of the credit applied and the debit invoice that it was applied to. If the credit is more than the debit invoice, the amount remaining can be applied to another invoice.

·Credit Un-applied: a user from your company terminated a payment associated with a debit invoice that the credit was applied to. The action comment identifies the amount of the terminated payment and the debit invoice where the credit was applied.

·Payment Settled Externally: the credit was dealt with outside of the OpenInvoice platform and a user from your company removed the credit from workflow. The credit is no longer available to be applied to debits.
